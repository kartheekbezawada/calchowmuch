**Q1: What is the present value of an annuity?**
A: The present value of an annuity is the value today of a series of regular future payments, discounted using a specific interest or discount rate.

**Q2: What is the difference between an ordinary annuity and an annuity due?**
A: In an ordinary annuity, payments occur at the end of each period, while in an annuity due, payments occur at the beginning of each period.

**Q3: Why is the present value of an annuity due higher than an ordinary annuity?**
A: An annuity due has a higher present value because payments are received earlier and are discounted for fewer periods.

**Q4: How do you calculate the present value of an ordinary annuity?**
A: The present value of an ordinary annuity is calculated by discounting each payment made at the end of each period back to today using the discount rate.

**Q5: How do you calculate the present value of an annuity due?**
A: The present value of an annuity due is calculated by adjusting the ordinary annuity present value to account for payments made at the beginning of each period.

**Q9: What happens if the discount rate is zero?**
