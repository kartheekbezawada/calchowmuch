**Q1: What is present value (PV)?**
A: Present value is the current worth of a future amount of money after accounting for the time value of money.

**Q2: Why is present value lower than future value?**
A: Because money available today can earn returns, a future amount is discounted to reflect lost earning potential.

**Q3: What discount rate should I use?**
A: Use a rate that reflects inflation, investment returns, or the opportunity cost relevant to your decision.

**Q4: How does the time period affect present value?**
A: Longer time periods increase discounting, which lowers the present value.

**Q5: What is compounding frequency?**
A: It defines how often the discount rate is applied, such as annually or monthly.

**Q6: Is present value used in investment decisions?**
A: Yes, it is commonly used to evaluate investments, loans, pensions, and future cash flows.

**Q7: Can present value be higher than future value?**
A: No, with a positive discount rate, present value will always be lower than future value.

**Q8: Does inflation affect present value?**
A: Yes, higher inflation increases discounting and reduces present value.

**Q9: What happens if the discount rate is zero?**
A: The present value equals the future value because no discounting is applied.
