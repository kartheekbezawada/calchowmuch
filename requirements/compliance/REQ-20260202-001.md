## Problem Statement

The current "How Much Can I Borrow" Explanation Pane (`explanation.html`) lacks a structured, intent-aligned design that clearly connects user inputs to outcomes in a transparent, lender-realistic way.

Users need a predictable pattern:
1. **Borrowing Summary** (Dynamic - driven by calculation data)
2. **Scenario Table** (Dynamic - showing all inputs and results)
3. **Explanation** (Static - how lenders assess borrowing)
4. **FAQ** (Static - common questions)

Sections 1 and 2 must be fully driven by Calculation Pane data rather than static text.

---

## Scope (What Must Be Built)

Rebuild `/public/calculators/loans/how-much-can-i-borrow/explanation.html` with four sections in exact order:

### Section 1: Initial Borrowing Summary (Dynamic) — H2 Header

**Requirements:**
- Generate summary dynamically using Calculation Pane outputs
- Must communicate that result is an estimate, not a guarantee
- Reference inputs conceptually (do NOT print raw numbers in prose)
- Mention: income, expenses, selected interest rate, loan term, affordability method
- Note that lenders may vary in final decisions
- Highlight the **Estimated Max Borrowing** as the final answer

**Rule:** This section describes the logic and result conceptually. Raw values appear ONLY in Section 2 table.

### Section 2: Scenario Summary Table (Dynamic) — H3 Header

**Requirements:**
- Auto-generate table from Calculation Pane data
- Table must update when inputs change and result is recalculated

**Table Contract:**

| Column | Source | Constraint |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Category | Fixed labels | As defined below |
| Your Value | Calculation Pane | Must pull live data |
| What it means | Static guidance | Short, neutral |

**Mandatory rows (in this order):**
1. Gross Annual Income
2. Net Monthly Income
3. Monthly Expenses
4. Monthly Debt Payments
5. Interest Rate (APR %)
6. Loan Term
7. Affordability Method
8. Income Multiple (if applicable)
9. Deposit (optional/conditional)
10. **Estimated Max Borrowing** ← Highlight as final answer

**Rule:** This table is the ONLY place where raw input values are shown.

### Section 3: General Explanation (Static) — H3 Header

**Requirements:**
- Plain language explanation of how lenders assess borrowing:
  - Income multiples
  - Affordability checks
  - Stress testing for rate rises
- Why results change when inputs change
- That different lenders may approve different amounts
- This text is standardized across all users

### Section 4: FAQ (Static) — H3 Header

**Requirements:**
Include at minimum these FAQs:

**Q1. Is this a guaranteed amount I can borrow?**  
No. This is only an estimate. Final approval depends on your credit score, employment stability, and the lender's policies.

**Q2. Why is my borrowing less than 5x my income?**  
Because lenders adjust for your expenses, existing debts, and interest rate stress tests.

**Q3. Can I borrow more with a longer loan term?**  
Often yes — a longer term can lower monthly payments and increase affordability, but you will pay more interest overall.

**Q4. Do different lenders give different amounts?**  
Yes. Some lenders are more flexible with expenses or income multiples than others.

**Q5. Should I speak to a broker?**  
If you are serious about buying, a broker can match you with the lender most likely to approve your application.

**Note:** Use this tool to understand your mortgage borrowing power, plan your deposit, and estimate how much house you can afford before applying.
