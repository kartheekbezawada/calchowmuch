# Balance Transfer Credit Card Calculator – Explanation and Guide

A balance transfer credit card calculator helps estimate whether transferring an existing credit card balance to a new card with a promotional interest rate could reduce the total interest you pay and shorten your repayment timeline. Many credit cards offer introductory balance transfer promotions where the interest rate is temporarily reduced, often to 0%, for a fixed number of months. During this promotional period, payments go directly toward reducing the principal balance rather than paying interest.

However, balance transfers often involve additional factors such as transfer fees, promotional interest periods, and post-promotion interest rates. This calculator combines those variables to estimate the total repayment cost and determine whether a balance transfer may provide meaningful savings.

## Example Balance Transfer Scenario

Suppose a credit card holder has the following balance:

- **Existing balance:** £8,500
- **Current APR:** 18.9%
- **Monthly payment:** £260

A new credit card offers:

- **0% promotional APR:** 12 months
- **Balance transfer fee:** 3%

Using these inputs, the calculator estimates the repayment timeline and total interest cost both with and without the balance transfer.

The balance transfer fee would be:

```
Transfer Fee = Balance × Transfer Fee %
```

**Example:**
```
Transfer Fee = 8,500 × 0.03
Transfer Fee = £255
```

The transferred balance becomes:

```
New Balance = Original Balance + Transfer Fee
New Balance = 8,500 + 255
New Balance = £8,755
```

Because the promotional APR is 0%, payments during the promotional period reduce the balance without additional interest charges.

## How Interest Is Calculated Without a Balance Transfer

If the balance remains on the original card, interest compounds based on the card's APR. Credit card interest is typically applied monthly, meaning the annual interest rate must be converted to a monthly rate.

### Monthly Interest Rate

```
Monthly Interest Rate = APR ÷ 12
```

**Example:**
```
APR = 18.9%
Monthly Rate = 18.9 ÷ 12
Monthly Rate ≈ 1.575%
```

Converted to decimal form:
```
Monthly Rate = 0.01575
```

### Monthly Interest Charge

Interest is applied to the remaining balance each month.

```
Interest = Current Balance × Monthly Interest Rate
```

**Example:**
```
Interest = 8,500 × 0.01575
Interest ≈ £133.88
```

This amount is added to the balance before the monthly payment is applied.

### Principal Reduction

After interest is calculated, the payment is applied. The portion of the payment that reduces the balance is:

```
Principal Reduction = Monthly Payment − Interest
```

**Example:**
```
Principal Reduction = 260 − 133.88
Principal Reduction ≈ £126.12
```

The new balance becomes:

```
New Balance = Previous Balance − Principal Reduction
```

This process repeats monthly until the balance reaches zero.

## How Interest Works During a Balance Transfer Promotion

If a balance is transferred to a card with a 0% promotional APR, interest is not applied during the promotional period. This means the entire payment goes toward reducing the principal balance.

**During the promotional period:**
- Interest = 0
- Principal Reduction = Monthly Payment

**Example:**
- Monthly Payment = £260
- **Balance Reduction = £260 per month**

This allows the balance to decrease significantly before interest begins again.

## After the Promotional Period Ends

Once the promotional period expires, the card's standard interest rate typically applies to the remaining balance. At that point, interest begins compounding again using the standard APR.

If the remaining balance after the promotional period is:
```
Remaining Balance = £5,635
```

and the standard APR is 18.9%, the monthly interest becomes:

```
Interest = Remaining Balance × Monthly Rate
Interest = 5,635 × 0.01575
Interest ≈ £88.71
```

From that point onward, the repayment process continues similarly to a standard credit card balance.

## How to Use the Balance Transfer Calculator

Using the calculator is straightforward. Enter the following values:

### Current Balance
This is the amount currently owed on the existing credit card.

### Current APR
The annual interest rate charged by the existing credit card.

### Balance Transfer Fee
Most balance transfers charge a fee between 3% and 5% of the transferred balance.

### Promotional APR
The temporary interest rate offered by the new card during the promotional period.

### Promotional Period
The number of months the promotional APR remains active.

### Monthly Payment
The amount you plan to pay toward the balance each month.

**After entering these values, the calculator estimates:**
- payoff timeline
- interest saved
- total repayment cost
- remaining balance after the promotional period

This allows you to compare whether transferring the balance provides meaningful financial savings.

## When a Balance Transfer Makes Sense

A balance transfer is usually beneficial when:

- the promotional APR is significantly lower than the current interest rate
- the balance transfer fee is relatively small
- the balance can be paid down substantially during the promotional period

If the balance can be completely repaid before the promotional period ends, the borrower may avoid interest entirely aside from the transfer fee.

However, if the balance remains large after the promotion expires, the savings may be smaller because the standard APR will begin applying again.
