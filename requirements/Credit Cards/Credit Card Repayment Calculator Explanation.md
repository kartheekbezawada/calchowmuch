# Credit Card Repayment Calculator Explanation

## Understanding the Key Inputs

Each input influences the repayment schedule and total interest cost.

### Card Balance

The card balance represents the outstanding amount currently owed to the credit card issuer. Interest is calculated on this balance each billing cycle until the full amount is repaid.

In this example, the starting balance is £8,500. Because interest is applied monthly, the balance will decrease slowly at first since a portion of each payment goes toward interest rather than reducing the principal.

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The APR is the yearly interest rate applied to the balance. Credit card interest is usually compounded monthly, meaning the annual rate must be converted into a monthly rate.

**Formula:**
```
Monthly Interest Rate = APR / 12
```

For an APR of 18.9%:
```
Monthly Rate = 18.9% ÷ 12
             ≈ 1.575% per month
```

Each month, interest is added to the remaining balance based on this rate.

### Monthly Payment

The monthly payment is the amount paid toward the credit card balance each month.

In this example:
- Base Monthly Payment = £260
- Extra Payment = £10
- **Total Monthly Payment = £270**

Making consistent payments above the minimum amount significantly accelerates debt repayment.

## How the Repayment Calculation Works

Credit card payoff calculations follow a standard amortization process.

Each month:
1. Interest is calculated on the remaining balance.
2. The payment is applied.
3. Part of the payment covers interest.
4. The remaining portion reduces the principal balance.

The basic formula used to calculate monthly interest is:

```
Interest = Current Balance × Monthly Interest Rate
```

For the first month in this example:
```
Interest = 8500 × 0.01575
         ≈ £133.88
```

After interest is applied, the payment is deducted.
```
Payment = £270
```

```
Principal Reduction = Payment − Interest
Principal Reduction = 270 − 133.88
                    ≈ £136.12
```

The remaining balance after the first payment becomes:
```
New Balance = 8500 − 136.12
            ≈ £8,363.88
```

This process repeats each month until the balance reaches zero.

## Why Interest Costs Add Up

Even though the payment amount remains fixed, the interest portion of each payment gradually decreases as the balance shrinks.

**At the beginning of the repayment period:**
- A larger share of each payment goes toward interest.
- The principal decreases slowly.

**Later in the repayment schedule:**
- Interest becomes smaller.
- More of the payment reduces the principal.

Because interest compounds monthly, early principal reduction has the greatest impact on total cost.

## How Extra Payments Change the Timeline

In this example, an additional £10 monthly payment was included. While £10 may seem small, extra payments reduce the balance faster and therefore decrease the amount of interest calculated each month.

Because interest is applied to the remaining balance, every pound paid early reduces future interest charges.

Over the entire repayment period, even small recurring extra payments can:
- shorten the repayment timeline
- reduce total interest
- lower the total amount paid

For example, increasing the extra payment to £50 or £100 per month could significantly shorten the payoff schedule.

## Strategies to Reduce Credit Card Interest

If you are trying to eliminate credit card debt faster, several strategies can help.

### Increase Monthly Payments
Paying more than the minimum required payment reduces principal faster and shortens the repayment period.

### Make Extra Payments When Possible
Occasional lump-sum payments, such as tax refunds or bonuses, can dramatically reduce the remaining balance.

### Lower the Interest Rate
Transferring balances to a lower APR credit card or consolidating debt into a lower-interest loan may reduce total interest costs.

### Avoid New Purchases
Adding new charges while trying to repay existing debt increases the balance and extends the repayment timeline.

## Why Planning Matters

Credit card debt can grow quickly because of compound interest. Without a repayment plan, balances may take many years to eliminate.

Using a repayment calculator helps visualize:
- how long repayment will take
- how interest accumulates over time
- how different payment strategies change the outcome

Seeing the full repayment timeline often encourages borrowers to increase payments when possible.

## Formulas for Credit Card Repayment Calculator

1. **Monthly Payment Used in the Calculation:**
   ```
   Total Monthly Payment = Monthly Payment + Extra Payment
   ```

2. **Converting APR to Monthly Interest Rate:**
   ```
   Monthly Interest Rate = APR ÷ 12
   ```

3. **Monthly Interest Calculation:**
   ```
   Interest = Current Balance × Monthly Interest Rate
   ```

4. **Principal Reduction Each Month:**
   ```
   Principal Reduction = Monthly Payment – Interest
   ```

5. **Updating the Remaining Balance:**
   ```
   New Balance = Previous Balance + Interest – Payment
   ```

6. **Determining the Payoff Horizon:**
   Continue monthly calculations until balance reaches zero

7. **Calculating Total Interest Paid:**
   Sum of all monthly interest charges over repayment period

8. **Total Amount Paid:**
   ```
   Total Paid = Original Balance + Total Interest
   ```

## Important Notes About the Calculation

This calculator provides estimates based on several simplifying assumptions:
- Interest rate remains constant
- Payments are made on time each month
- No additional purchases are added to the balance
- Compounding occurs monthly
