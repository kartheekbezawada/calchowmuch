Frequently Asked Questions

01

How does a leasing calculator estimate monthly payment?

It combines monthly depreciation and finance charge using cap cost, residual, term, and money factor.

02

What is residual value in a lease?

Residual is the estimated vehicle value at lease end, used to determine depreciation portion.

03

Can I enter residual as amount or percent?

Yes. Use the residual toggle to switch between amount and percent modes.

04

What does money factor represent?

Money factor represents lease finance cost and is analogous to an interest rate factor.

05

How does upfront payment affect lease cost?

A higher upfront payment lowers cap cost, which can reduce monthly payment and finance charge.

06

Can term be switched between years and months?

Yes. Use the term unit toggle and recalculate to compare different lease durations.

07

Does the calculator recalculate while I drag sliders?

No. Results and explanation update only when you click Calculate Lease.

08

Why can residual not exceed vehicle price?

Residual above vehicle price would imply negative depreciation and unrealistic lease economics.

09

What is included in total lease cost?

Total lease cost includes upfront payment plus all monthly lease payments over the term.

10

Are these lender-specific lease quotes?

No. These are model-based estimates and may differ from lender fees, taxes, and terms.
