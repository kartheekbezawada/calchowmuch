Loan-to-Value (LTV) Calculator

Loan-to-value (LTV) compares your loan amount to the property value. It is one of the key metrics lenders use to price risk and decide which mortgage products you can access.

Lower LTV usually means better rates and wider lender choice. Use this calculator to test different deposit or loan combinations and see where you land in typical lending bands.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is LTV in a mortgage?

LTV is the loan amount divided by the property value, expressed as a percentage. Example: 280,000 loan on a 350,000 property gives 80% LTV.

02

Is lower LTV always better?

Generally yes. Lower LTV often unlocks better rates and more lender options because the lender is taking less risk.

03

How do I reduce my LTV?

You can reduce LTV by increasing your deposit, paying down the loan balance, or buying at a lower property price.

04

What LTV do first-time buyers usually target?

Many first-time buyers aim for 90% to 95% LTV, but products and pricing usually improve as you move toward 85%, 80%, or lower.

05

Does property value change my LTV over time?

Yes. If the property value rises and your loan stays the same or falls, your LTV improves. If value drops, LTV can worsen.

06

What happens above 95% LTV?

Lending options are usually very limited and rates can be significantly higher. Some lenders may require additional checks or decline entirely.

07

Does LTV affect remortgage options?

Yes. Better LTV bands often give access to stronger remortgage deals. This is why many borrowers monitor LTV before a fixed rate expires.

08

Can I use deposit percent instead of amount?

Yes. This calculator supports both. Enter the deposit as a percentage to quickly test different LTV outcomes across price points.

09

Is LTV the only affordability factor?

No. Lenders also check income, existing debts, credit profile, and monthly affordability. LTV is one major factor, but not the only one.

10

How often should I recalculate LTV?

Recheck whenever your balance changes materially, your property value changes, or you are planning a purchase or remortgage decision.
