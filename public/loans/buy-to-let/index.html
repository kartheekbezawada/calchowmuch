Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is a buy-to-let mortgage?

A buy-to-let mortgage is a loan designed for purchasing property to rent out to tenants rather than for personal occupation.

02

How does a buy-to-let mortgage work?

You borrow to purchase a rental property, collect rent, and cover mortgage and operating costs. Remaining surplus is net cashflow.

03

What deposit is usually required?

Most lenders expect a larger deposit than residential mortgages, often around 20-25% or higher.

04

Why compare interest-only and repayment?

Interest-only usually improves monthly cashflow, while repayment builds equity faster but increases monthly mortgage cost.

05

What does stress coverage mean?

Stress coverage compares effective rent to mortgage cost, helping you gauge resilience under tighter conditions.

06

How should advanced options be used?

Use advanced assumptions to model vacancy, fees, maintenance, and rent growth for more realistic planning outcomes.
