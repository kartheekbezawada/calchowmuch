How this car loan calculator works

The calculator combines your price, trade-in, fees, and sales tax to estimate the amount you finance. It then applies a standard amortization formula to compute monthly payments and the year-by-year balance.

Car loan formula

Amount financed = vehicle price - down payment - trade-in + fees + sales tax

Monthly payment is based on the financed amount, APR, and loan term using a fixed-rate amortization schedule.

Inputs and assumptions

  • Vehicle price, down payment (amount or percent), and trade-in value.
  • Dealer fees and sales tax percentage.
  • APR and loan term in years.

Results are estimates. Tax rules and fees vary by location and lender, so use your lender's final disclosures for exact numbers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the amount financed calculated?

It is the vehicle price minus down payment and trade-in, plus dealer fees and sales tax.

Does a larger down payment reduce monthly payments?

Yes. A larger down payment lowers the amount financed, which reduces monthly payment and total interest.

What does total cost include?

Total cost equals all monthly payments plus your down payment for the vehicle.

How does sales tax affect the loan?

Sales tax increases the amount financed, which raises both monthly payment and total interest.
