Frequently Asked Questions

01

What does an offset account do?

It reduces the balance charged interest by linking savings to your mortgage, which lowers interest while keeping your repayment schedule active.

02

Why does the payment stay the same in this model?

The calculator keeps baseline monthly payment fixed so savings show up as lower interest and faster principal reduction.

03

What is Full Offset mode?

Full mode applies the entire offset savings balance against mortgage balance for interest calculations each month.

04

What is Partial / Average mode?

Partial mode applies 50% of savings to represent daily balance fluctuations in the offset account.

05

How does monthly contribution help?

Regular contribution grows the offset balance over time, increasing the amount shielded from interest and improving savings.

06

What does effective balance mean?

Effective balance is the estimated balance actually accruing interest after offset savings are applied.

07

Why compare monthly and yearly tables?

Monthly view shows precision and trend, while yearly view helps assess strategic savings over a longer horizon.

08

Can offset savings reduce loan term?

Yes. Lower interest means more principal is repaid sooner, which can shorten payoff time.

09

Does this replace lender statements?

No. It is a planning model. Your lender’s compounding rules, product terms, and posting timings may differ.

10

How should I use this for decision-making?

Test several savings balances and contribution levels, then compare interest saved, payoff time reduction, and yearly trend consistency.
