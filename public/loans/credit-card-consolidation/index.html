Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is credit card debt consolidation?

A: Credit card debt consolidation combines multiple card balances into one loan with a fixed APR and repayment term so you can simplify payments and compare total cost.

Q: How does a consolidation loan reduce interest costs?

A: If the consolidation APR is lower than your current card APR, a larger share of each payment goes to principal, which can reduce total interest.

Q: When is consolidation not worth it?

A: Consolidation may not be worth it when fees are high, the new APR is not lower enough, or the new term is too long and increases total cost.

Q: How are consolidation fees handled in the calculation?

A: Fees are added to the balance financed by the consolidation loan, which can raise monthly payment and total cost.

Q: What APR can I expect for a consolidation loan?

A: APR depends on credit profile, lender policy, loan amount, and term. Compare multiple offers to estimate realistic rates.

Q: Does consolidation affect my credit score?

A: It can. Opening a new loan and paying off cards may change utilization and account mix. Long-term impact depends on repayment behavior.

Q: Can I consolidate cards with different APRs?

A: Yes. This calculator uses one average current APR input, so combine your card balances and use a reasonable weighted average APR.

Q: What happens if I miss a payment on a consolidation loan?

A: Missing payments may trigger fees, possible APR changes, and credit-score damage, which can reduce or erase expected savings.

Q: Does this calculator include origination fees?

A: Yes. Enter origination or setup fees in Consolidation Fees so the comparison reflects the full financed amount.

Q: How do I compare consolidation vs. balance transfer?

A: Compare APR, fees, repayment term, and payoff discipline. Use both calculators and select the option with lower total paid and realistic monthly payment.
