FAQ

What does the Energy-Based Nap Selector do?

It maps your selected goal and start time to a deterministic nap duration and wake-up recommendation with alternatives.

How are Quick, Strong, and Full goals mapped?

Quick is 15 minutes, Strong is 25 minutes, and Full is 90 minutes.

Why can a late-night recommendation become shorter?

Long late-night naps can delay bedtime, so inferred Full recommendations are shortened to reduce disruption risk.

When does the tool keep a full 90-minute nap at night?

If you explicitly choose Full, the recommendation stays at 90 minutes and a warning is shown instead of forcing a downgrade.

Does it account for my personal bedtime habits?

No. The output is deterministic and does not model individual routines.

Which time buckets are used?

Results are classified into Day, Afternoon, and Night buckets for recommendation context.

Can shift workers use this calculator?

Yes. It uses local clock time and works for daytime or overnight schedules.

Does the calculator set alarms automatically?

No. It provides wake-up targets that you can copy into your own alarm app.

Will changing inputs auto-calculate?

No. Results update only when you click Calculate.

Is this medical advice?

No. It is a practical planning tool and does not replace professional medical guidance.
