Explanation

The selector uses fixed mapping so each goal always corresponds to a deterministic nap length. It then computes wake-up time from your start time and selected or adjusted duration.

For late-night starts, inferred long naps are shortened to reduce the risk of pushing bedtime too late. If you explicitly choose Full at night, the duration remains 90 minutes, but a warning is shown so the tradeoff is clear.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Energy-Based Nap Selector do?

It recommends a nap length based on your selected goal and start time, then shows wake-up times for the primary and alternative options.

How are Quick, Strong, and Full goals mapped?

Quick maps to 15 minutes, Strong maps to 25 minutes, and Full maps to 90 minutes.

Why does the recommendation change at night?

Late-night long naps can disrupt normal sleep timing, so inferred Full goals at night are downgraded to a shorter recommendation.

When is a Full nap not downgraded?

If you explicitly choose Full, the tool keeps the 90-minute recommendation and shows a warning instead of downgrading.

Does this calculator account for bedtime routines?

No. It applies deterministic nap-duration rules and does not model personal bedtime habits.

What time buckets are used for warnings?

The calculator groups time into Day, Afternoon, and Night buckets and applies late-night warnings to long naps.

Can I use the selector for shift work?

Yes. The logic is based on local clock time and works for any schedule, including overnight routines.

Does the calculator set an alarm for me?

No. It gives wake-up times that you can use to set an alarm manually.

Will changing inputs recalculate instantly?

No. After page load, results update only when you click Calculate.

Is this tool medical advice?

No. It is a planning aid and does not diagnose sleep conditions or replace professional medical guidance.
