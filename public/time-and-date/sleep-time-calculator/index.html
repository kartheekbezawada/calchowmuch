FAQ

How many hours of sleep do I need?

Most adults do best with 7 to 9 hours per night, or about 5 to 6 cycles.

What is a sleep cycle?

A sleep cycle is a repeating pattern of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep that often lasts about 90 minutes.

Is 6 hours of sleep enough?

Six hours is roughly 4 cycles. Some people manage short-term, but many perform better with 7 or more hours consistently.

Why are multiple sleep times shown?

The calculator shows 4-cycle, 5-cycle, and 6-cycle options so you can choose a schedule that fits your day.

Why might I still feel tired after following the suggestions?

Cycle length varies by person and by night, and factors like stress, caffeine, sleep debt, or sleep disorders can affect how rested you feel.

Does this calculator account for time to fall asleep?

Yes. It includes a short fall-asleep buffer before the first sleep cycle begins.

Can I use this calculator if I work night shifts?

Yes. Enter your target wake-up or fall-asleep time regardless of the hour and the calculator will handle the rollover.

What happens if I wake up in the middle of a sleep cycle?

Waking during deep sleep can cause short-term grogginess and reduced alertness, often called sleep inertia.

Should I go to bed at the same time every night?

A consistent bedtime can help regulate your body clock and improve sleep quality over time.

Is it better to get 4 or 5 sleep cycles?

Five cycles, about 7.5 hours, is generally better for most adults than 4 cycles unless time is limited.
