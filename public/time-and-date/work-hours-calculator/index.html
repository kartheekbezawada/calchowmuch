How many work hours are in this shift or week?

Use this calculator to total single shifts, split shifts, or weekly schedules with unpaid breaks removed and overnight work handled correctly.

Current example: this single shift setup totals 8:00 hours, or 8.00 decimal hours, after a 30-minute unpaid break.

How to Guide

  1. Choose Single Shift, Split Shift, or Weekly Total based on how you track work time.
  2. Enter each start and end time, then turn on Ends next day for overnight work.
  3. Select the unpaid break deduction that should come off the worked total.
  4. Click Calculate to see the HH:MM total, decimal hours, and any per-segment or per-day lines.

How to use work-hour totals for real schedules

HH:MM is the easiest format for checking whether the shift looks right at a glance. Decimal hours are more useful when you need to compare the result with payroll software, a rota, or a timesheet entry. Seeing both formats side by side reduces the usual conversion mistakes that happen when people try to translate minutes into decimals in their heads.

Single Shift mode is usually enough when you have one clean start and finish time. Split Shift mode is better when work is broken into two segments and the unpaid gap should not count as time worked. Weekly Total helps when you need to add several days together before you check overtime or submit a full-week timesheet.

  • HH:MM view: quickest format for sense-checking the shift total.
  • Decimal view: easier to compare with payroll, billing, or spreadsheet entries.
  • Weekly mode: useful when one day alone does not tell the full hours story.

FAQ

Can I calculate hours for a night shift?

Yes. Enable Ends next day when your shift ends after midnight.

Does this include paid breaks?

No. Only unpaid breaks that you choose are deducted from the worked total.

Why do I see both HH:MM and decimal hours?

HH:MM is easier to read, while decimal hours are commonly used for payroll and timesheets.

Does it round time?

No. It uses the exact inputs you enter, so employer rounding rules need to be applied separately.
