FAQ

What is the difference between regular hours and overtime hours?

Regular hours are counted up to your limit. Overtime hours are the remaining hours above that limit.

Which overtime rule should I choose?

Use the rule that matches your timesheet policy. Weekly covers weekly caps, Daily covers daily caps, and Daily + Weekly checks both.

How does Daily + Weekly avoid double counting overtime?

The calculator compares the daily and weekly overtime totals, then keeps the larger overtime result so the same hours are not counted twice.

Can I calculate overtime for split shifts?

Yes. Split-shift segments are combined for the day, then the break deduction and overtime rule are applied.

What does Ends next day do?

It treats the end time as the next calendar day so overnight shifts are counted correctly.

What is night overtime in this calculator?

Night overtime is overtime that falls inside your chosen night window. It is a classification only, not a pay-rate calculation.

Does this calculator round time the way my employer does?

It can if you pick the same rounding option. If your employer uses a different method, results may differ.

Can I set a custom week like Sunday-Saturday or Friday-Thursday?

Yes. Weekly mode lets you choose the week start day and week start date, then labels the next six days automatically.

Does the calculator save my hours?

No. It runs in your browser and does not store your inputs.

How are breaks handled in overtime calculations?

Unpaid breaks are deducted from worked time before the calculator splits hours into regular, overtime, and night-overtime buckets.
