Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many sleep cycles should I aim for?

A: Many adults feel best with 5 or 6 cycles (about 7.5 to 9 hours), but personal needs vary.

Q: What is a sleep cycle?

A: A sleep cycle is a repeating pattern of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep that often lasts about 90 minutes.

Q: Why are 4, 5, and 6 cycles shown?

A: These options balance schedule flexibility with enough total sleep for most adults.

Q: Does this calculator assume it takes time to fall asleep?

A: Yes. In bedtime mode, it adds a fixed 15-minute buffer before sleep cycles begin.

Q: Can I use this if I wake during the night?

A: Yes. Re-enter the new bedtime and calculate again for updated wake-up suggestions.

Q: Why might I feel groggy even after a suggested wake-up time?

A: Cycle length varies by person, so timing can still feel off on some nights.

Q: Does daylight saving time affect the result?

A: It can, because clock changes can shift local wall-clock wake-up times.

Q: Is this calculator a medical tool?

A: No. It is a planning aid and does not diagnose sleep or health conditions.

Q: Should I choose 5 or 6 cycles?

A: Choose the option that fits your schedule while still leaving enough rest time.

Q: What if my schedule allows only 4 cycles?

A: 4 cycles can work occasionally, but many adults feel better with longer sleep when possible.
