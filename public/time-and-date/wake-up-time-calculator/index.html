FAQ

How many sleep cycles should I aim for?

Many adults feel best with 5 or 6 cycles, or about 7.5 to 9 hours, but personal needs vary.

What is a sleep cycle?

A sleep cycle is a repeating pattern of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM sleep that often lasts about 90 minutes.

Why are 4, 5, and 6 cycles shown?

These options give a practical range of wake-up times so you can balance sleep duration with your schedule.

Does this calculator assume it takes time to fall asleep?

Yes. In bedtime mode, it adds a 15-minute buffer before sleep cycles begin.

Can I use this if I wake during the night?

Yes. Re-enter the new bedtime and calculate again for updated wake-up suggestions.

Why might I feel groggy even after a suggested wake-up time?

Cycle length varies by person and by night, so a suggested wake-up time can still feel off sometimes.

Does daylight saving time affect the result?

It can, because clock changes can shift local wall-clock wake-up times.

Is this calculator a medical tool?

No. It is a planning aid and does not diagnose sleep or health conditions.

Should I choose 5 or 6 cycles?

Choose the option that fits your schedule while still leaving enough total sleep time.

What if my schedule allows only 4 cycles?

Four cycles can work occasionally, but many adults feel better with longer sleep when possible.
