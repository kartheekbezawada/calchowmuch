Margin Calculator

Calculate gross margin %, profit, and selling price from cost and margin targets.

Cost + Price -> Margin % Cost + Margin % -> Selling Price

Explanation

A margin calculator helps you find gross margin %, profit, and the selling price needed to reach a target margin.

Gross margin measures profit as a percentage of the selling price. This margin calculator can calculate gross margin % from cost and price, or calculate the selling price needed for a desired gross margin.

It is commonly used for pricing products, checking profitability, and planning margins in retail and business.

Explanation

Gross margin shows how much of the selling price is profit after covering the cost. To calculate gross margin %, subtract cost from selling price to get profit, then divide profit by the selling price and multiply by 100.

For example, if cost is 60 and the selling price is 100, profit is 40 and gross margin is (40 / 100) x 100 = 40%.

To find the selling price needed for a target gross margin, divide cost by (1 - margin). For example, if cost is 60 and the target margin is 40%, the selling price is 60 / (1 - 0.40) = 100.

If the selling price is zero, margin cannot be calculated. If the margin is 100%, the selling price cannot be calculated because division by zero is not possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is gross margin?

A: Gross margin is profit expressed as a percentage of the selling price.

Q: How do you calculate gross margin %?

A: Subtract cost from price, divide by price, then multiply by 100.

Q: What is the gross margin formula?

A: The formula is ((P - C) / P) x 100.

Q: How do you calculate profit?

A: Profit equals selling price minus cost.

Q: How do you calculate selling price from cost and margin %?

A: Divide cost by (1 - margin/100).

Q: Is margin the same as markup?

A: No, margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

Q: Can gross margin be negative?

A: Yes, if the selling price is lower than the cost, profit is negative and margin is negative.

Q: What happens if the selling price is zero?

A: Gross margin is undefined because you cannot divide by zero.

Q: Can margin be 100%?

A: A 100% margin would require zero cost or an undefined selling price in this formula.

Q: Where is gross margin used in real life?

A: It is used for product pricing, profitability analysis, and business planning.
