How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

The calculator converts a bonus (as a percentage or flat amount) into a dollar figure, adds it to your base salary, and shows total compensation with the effective bonus percentage.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your base annual salary.
  2. Choose percent mode (e.g., 10%) or amount mode (e.g., $8,000).
  3. Enter the bonus value.
  4. Click Calculate to see bonus amount, total compensation, and bonus percentage.

Key Insight

A 10% bonus looks generous, but if it replaces stable base pay, it is riskier. Compare two offers: $85K base vs. $78K + 15% bonus ($89,700 total). If the bonus hits only 50% of target, the second offer drops to $83,850 — less than the guaranteed salary. Always ask whether a bonus is guaranteed or at-risk before counting on it.

Real-World Example

Aisha is evaluating two offers. Offer A: $85,000 base with no bonus. Offer B: $78,000 base with a target 15% bonus. At full target, Offer B pays $89,700 — nearly $5K more.

But her research shows the company’s average bonus payout is 70% of target. At 70%, the bonus drops to $8,190, making total comp $86,190 — only $1,190 more than the guaranteed $85K. She uses the calculator to model 50%, 70%, and 100% scenarios before deciding.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a bonus from salary?

Multiply your base salary by the bonus percentage. For example, $80,000 × 10% = $8,000 bonus.

Is a bonus better than a raise?

A raise increases your salary permanently, while a bonus is usually one-time or annual. Over multiple years, a raise compounds; a bonus does not.

What if my bonus is not guaranteed?

Model multiple scenarios (50%, 70%, 100% of target) to see the realistic range. Do not plan fixed expenses around an at-risk bonus.

How do I compare two offers with different bonus structures?

Calculate total compensation for each (base + bonus) and compare. Also check whether each bonus is guaranteed or performance-based.

What is a typical bonus percentage?

Target bonuses range widely: 5–10% for individual contributors, 15–25% for managers, and 30%+ for senior executives at many companies.

Should I include the bonus in my monthly budget?

Only if it is guaranteed. For at-risk bonuses, budget on base salary alone and treat the bonus as savings or extra.

Does this include taxes on bonuses?

No. All figures are gross. Bonuses are often withheld at a higher rate, so your net bonus may be smaller than expected.

Is my data saved or sent anywhere?

No. All calculations run locally in your browser. Nothing is stored or transmitted.
