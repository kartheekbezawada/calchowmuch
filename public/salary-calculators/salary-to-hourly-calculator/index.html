How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

You enter an annual salary and your work schedule — hours per week and weeks per year — and the calculator shows your effective hourly rate along with weekly, biweekly, and monthly pay. Change the schedule and every figure recalculates instantly.

This is the tool to use when you want to compare two salary offers by what they actually pay per hour of your time, not just by the annual number.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the annual salary from your offer letter, contract, or current pay stub.
  2. Set your expected or actual hours per week — be honest about whether the role runs over 40.
  3. Confirm paid weeks per year (typically 52 unless you have unpaid leave).
  4. Click Calculate and check the hourly rate against your expectations or another offer.

Key Insight

A $75,000 salary pays $36.06 per hour with 40-hour weeks — but only $32.05 per hour with 45-hour weeks. That 5-hour difference costs you $4.01 on every hour worked, the equivalent of over $8,300 per year in uncompensated time.

Real-World Example

Dana is a project manager earning $78,000 per year. At a standard 40-hour week, that works out to $37.50 per hour. But her team regularly runs 50-hour weeks during product launches — and those extra hours are unpaid.

  • At 40 hrs/wk: $78,000 ÷ 2,080 = $37.50/hr
  • At 50 hrs/wk: $78,000 ÷ 2,600 = $30.00/hr

Same salary, two different realities. Those 10 extra unpaid hours per week effectively cost Dana $7.50 per hour — or roughly $19,500 a year in uncompensated overtime. Running the calculator with her real hours reveals what the offer letter does not.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert my salary to an hourly rate?

Divide annual salary by weeks per year, then by hours per week. For example, $75,000 ÷ 52 ÷ 40 = $36.06/hr.

Why does my hourly rate drop when I add more hours per week?

The annual salary stays the same, but it's divided across more hours. More hours with the same pay always lowers the per-hour rate.

A $75K salary — what's that per hour?

At a standard 40-hour, 52-week schedule, $75,000 per year equals $36.06 per hour. With longer weeks, the hourly rate drops proportionally.

Why does monthly pay stay the same even when hourly pay changes?

Monthly pay is annual salary divided by 12 — it doesn't depend on hours. Hourly pay depends on both salary and schedule, so it moves when hours change.

How do I compare two salaried offers using hourly rate?

Enter each salary with its expected weekly hours. The role with the higher hourly rate values your time more — even if its annual salary is lower.

What if my hours vary by season or project?

Use your average weekly hours over the past 6–12 months. The calculator assumes consistent hours, so an average gives the most realistic effective rate.

Does this include overtime premium pay?

No. This divides salary by total hours worked. If your extra hours should legally earn 1.5× pay, use the Overtime Pay Calculator separately.

Can I use this for part-time salaried roles?

Yes. Enter your actual hours and weeks. A part-time salaried role with fewer hours will show a higher effective hourly rate.

Should I negotiate based on annual salary or hourly rate?

Both matter, but hourly rate reveals whether the schedule makes the salary fair. A high salary with extreme hours can be a worse deal per hour.

Is my data saved or sent anywhere?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser. Nothing is stored or transmitted to any server.
