How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

The calculator estimates commission from a sales amount and rate (or flat amount), adds any base pay, and shows total earnings with the effective commission rate.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the sales amount for the period.
  2. Choose rate mode (e.g., 6%) or amount mode (e.g., $4,800).
  3. Enter the commission value.
  4. Optional: enter base pay to see total earnings.
  5. Click Calculate to see commission, total earnings, and effective rate.

Key Insight

Commission pay amplifies good months AND bad months. At a 6% rate with $3,500 base, a $60K sales month earns $7,100. But a $15K slump month earns only $4,400. If your fixed expenses are $5,500, one slow month creates a $1,100 shortfall. Always stress-test the worst-case month, not just the average.

Real-World Example

Reena is a SaaS account executive with a $42,000 base ($3,500/month) and a 6% commission on closed deals. In her best quarter, she closed $210,000 in deals — $12,600 in commission, for total quarterly earnings of $23,100.

But in Q1, she closed only $45,000 — just $2,700 in commission, dropping quarterly earnings to $13,200. Her income swung by nearly $10,000 per quarter based on pipeline alone. She uses the calculator to model best-case, average, and worst-case months before committing to a budget.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate commission?

Multiply the sales amount by the commission rate. For example, $80,000 in sales at 6% = $4,800 commission.

Is commission better than a fixed salary?

It depends on your risk tolerance and sales consistency. Commission has higher upside but also higher downside. Strong sellers in stable markets often prefer it.

What is a good commission rate?

Rates vary by industry: 5–10% is common in real estate and SaaS, 1–3% in high-volume retail, and 15–25% or more in specialized services.

How do I budget on commission income?

Base your fixed expenses on your worst realistic month, not your average. Treat above-average months as savings or debt-payoff opportunities.

Can I add base pay to see total earnings?

Yes. Enter base pay in the optional field and the calculator adds it to commission for total earnings.

What is the effective commission rate?

It is the commission amount divided by sales. Useful when comparing different pay plans that use different payout structures.

Does this include taxes?

No. All figures are gross. Commission income is taxed as ordinary income in most cases.

Is my data saved or sent anywhere?

No. All calculations run locally in your browser. Nothing is stored or transmitted.
