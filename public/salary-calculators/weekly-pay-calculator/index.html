How it works

What this weekly pay calculator shows

This route keeps the weekly answer first. Use the default total-hours formula for a direct estimate, or switch to split hours when you want to enter regular and overtime time separately.

  • Standard weekly pay: hourly rate × total weekly hours
  • Split-hours weekly pay: regular hours × rate + overtime hours × rate × multiplier
  • Annualized pay: weekly pay × weeks per year

Worked example

At $25.00 per hour for 40 weekly hours, weekly pay is $1,000.00. If that same result is annualized over 52 weeks, the annualized pay is $52,000.00.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate weekly pay?

Multiply the hourly rate by total weekly hours, or use split regular and overtime hours.

Can this include overtime hours?

Yes. Split-hours mode adds an editable overtime multiplier without making it the default.

Can I annualize the weekly result?

Yes. The annualized estimate multiplies weekly pay by the weeks-per-year value you enter.

Does this work for part-time schedules?

Yes. Enter the hours that match your own workweek.

Does the result include tax deductions?

No. This route estimates gross weekly pay only.
