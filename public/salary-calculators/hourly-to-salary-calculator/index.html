Pay conversion guide

Find out what your hourly rate is really worth per year

You know your hourly rate but need to understand what it adds up to over a year — for budgeting, comparing against a salaried offer, or negotiating a raise. This calculator multiplies your hourly wage by your schedule and breaks the result into annual, monthly, biweekly, and weekly gross pay.

How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

You enter an hourly rate and your work schedule — hours per week and paid weeks per year — and the calculator shows your estimated annual salary along with monthly, biweekly, and weekly totals. Change the schedule and every figure updates instantly.

This is the tool to use when someone quotes you an hourly wage and you need to know what that means as a yearly income or monthly budget figure.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Gather your hourly rate from an offer letter, pay stub, or contract.
  2. Confirm your expected weekly hours — check whether the role is 37.5, 40, or something else.
  3. Set paid weeks per year: 52 if fully salaried, lower if you have unpaid leave or seasonal gaps.
  4. Click Calculate and compare the annual result to a salaried offer or your current income.

Key Insight

A $35/hr freelance rate at 30 hours per week earns $50,400 per year. A full-time offer at $58,000 sounds higher — but it drops your effective rate to $27.88/hr and adds 640 extra working hours. Higher annual pay does not always mean better pay per hour of your life.

Real-World Example

Priya freelances at $35 per hour, averaging 30 hours per week for 48 weeks a year (she takes a month of unpaid breaks). A company just offered her a full-time role at $58,000 per year with a 40-hour week.

  • Priya's freelance annual: $35 × 30 hrs × 48 wks = $50,400/yr → $4,200/mo
  • Full-time offer: $58,000/yr → $4,833/mo → $27.88/hr

The salaried role pays $7,600 more per year, but Priya's hourly rate drops from $35 to $27.88 and she works 640 more hours. The trade-off: more guaranteed income but less per-hour value and less flexibility. Running both through the calculator makes the comparison visible.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert an hourly rate to annual salary?

Multiply your hourly rate by hours per week, then by paid weeks per year. For example, $25/hr × 40 hrs × 52 wks = $52,000.

Why does my annual salary change when I adjust hours or weeks?

Annual salary equals hourly rate × hours × weeks. Fewer hours or fewer paid weeks directly lower the total — even if the hourly rate stays the same.

Is a $20/hr job really $41,600 per year?

At 40 hours per week for 52 weeks, yes. But if you work fewer weeks or shorter hours, the annual total drops. Always confirm the schedule behind the number.

How do I know if a salaried offer is better than my hourly rate?

Convert both to the same basis. Enter your hourly rate here, then divide the salary offer by its expected hours. Compare the effective hourly rates side by side.

What if I work different hours each week?

Use your average weekly hours over the past few months. The calculator assumes consistent hours, so an average gives the most realistic annual estimate.

Do freelancers and contractors use this differently?

Set weeks per year to the number of weeks you actually bill. If you take unpaid breaks or have gaps between contracts, use a lower number than 52.

What schedule should I use if my employer offers unpaid time off?

Subtract unpaid weeks from 52. Two weeks of unpaid leave means 50 paid weeks — which lowers your annual total even if the hourly rate stays the same.

Why are monthly and biweekly pay different amounts?

Monthly divides annual pay by 12. Biweekly divides by 26. They split the same total into different-sized pieces, which affects paycheck timing and budget planning.

Does this include overtime pay?

No. This converts your base hourly rate only. For overtime calculations at 1.5× or 2× rates, use the Overtime Pay Calculator.

Is my data saved or sent anywhere?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser. Nothing is stored or transmitted to any server.
