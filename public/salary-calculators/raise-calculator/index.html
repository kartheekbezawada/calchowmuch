Pay increase guide

How a raise calculator shows the real size of your next pay increase

This raise calculator compares your current salary, raise amount, and new salary in one view. It works with either a percentage raise or a flat amount, so you can check the impact before a review, offer, or negotiation.

The main value is clarity. A percentage can sound large, but the dollar change shows whether the raise is actually meaningful for your budget.

How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

The calculator turns a raise into two clear answers: the raise amount and the new salary. It also shows the resulting percent increase for context.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your current salary.
  2. Choose percent mode or amount mode.
  3. Enter the raise percentage or flat raise amount.
  4. Click Calculate to see the raise amount and new salary.
  5. Compare the result with your monthly budget and longer-term goals.

How to Interpret Results

Result How to use it
Raise amount Shows the direct dollar increase in gross pay.
New salary Shows your updated gross annual pay.
Percent increase Helps compare offers with different raise styles.

Use the raise amount to judge immediate impact and the new salary to review the bigger picture. If two offers use different raise formats, the percent result makes comparison easier.

Formula breakdown: in percent mode, raise amount equals current salary times raise percent. In amount mode, the raise amount is the number you enter, and new salary equals current salary plus raise amount.

Real-World Example

With a current salary of $60,000.00 and a 5% raise, the raise amount is $3,000.00. The new salary becomes $63,000.00.

Raise Impact

Percent mode grows with your current salary, so the same percentage can mean very different money for two people. Flat raises do the reverse and change the effective percentage based on where you start.

That is why both the dollar amount and the percentage matter. One tells you what hits your budget, while the other helps you compare with other offers.

Grow Your Wealth

Raises work best when part of the increase is captured early. Moving a slice of the extra pay to savings or investing can improve your finances without changing your baseline lifestyle much.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate a raise?

You can either add a flat raise amount to current salary or multiply current salary by the raise percentage.

Can I use a percent raise or a flat raise amount?

Yes. The calculator supports both modes.

Does this show my new salary after the raise?

Yes. The main result is the new salary after applying the raise.

Can I see the raise amount separately?

Yes. The calculator also shows the raise amount and the resulting percent increase.

Does this include taxes?

No. It estimates gross pay changes only.
