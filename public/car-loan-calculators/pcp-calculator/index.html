How to Guide

What a PCP Calculator Helps You Judge

PCP finance usually lowers the monthly payment by deferring part of the vehicle value to the end of the contract. That can make the offer look more affordable than a standard car loan, but it also creates a larger final decision point at the end.

This calculator helps you see whether the lower monthly payment is worth the size of the GFV and the final payment obligation.

How to Enter the PCP Inputs

Start with the car price and deposit, then enter APR, term, GFV, and any option fee. Those inputs determine how much principal is repaid monthly and how much stays deferred until the contract ends.

  • Deposit reduces the financed amount from day one.
  • GFV is the deferred value still due if you buy the vehicle at the end.
  • Option fee is added to the end-of-term purchase cost.
  • APR and term shape both installment size and overall borrowing cost.

Why PCP Monthly Payments Are Usually Lower

PCP does not repay the full vehicle cost during the monthly schedule. Instead, part of the value is left as GFV, so each monthly installment repays less principal than a standard loan would over the same period.

Lower monthly payments do not always mean lower total cost. The deferred amount still has to be cleared later if you want to keep the car.

How Final Payment Is Built

The end-of-term payment is not just GFV. It also includes any option fee required to complete the purchase.

Final payment = GFV + Option fee

This is the number many buyers underestimate when they compare PCP against other finance types.

Worked Example

Imagine a 28,000 vehicle with a 4,000 deposit, 8% APR, a 4-year term, and a large GFV. The monthly payment may look much lower than a normal auto loan, but the contract can still leave a substantial final payment if you choose to own the car at the end.

That means PCP works best when you are comfortable with the end-of-term choice, not just the monthly figure.

How Input Changes Affect the Result

Change Typical Effect
Higher deposit Lowers financed amount and usually lowers both monthly payment and total cost.
Higher GFV Lowers monthly payment but increases the final amount left to settle.
Higher option fee Raises the end-of-term payment if you buy the vehicle.
Longer term May reduce monthly payment but can increase total interest paid.

How to Interpret the Outputs

Monthly payment helps with budgeting, but the most important PCP outputs are the final payment, total interest, and total payable. If those are too high, the finance may only look cheaper because cost is being pushed to the end.

Use the cost breakdown table to see exactly where the money goes before deciding whether PCP is better than a standard loan or lease alternative.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Judging the deal on monthly payment alone.
  • Ignoring the combined impact of GFV and option fee.
  • Assuming a high GFV automatically makes the finance cheaper overall.
  • Not testing how a larger deposit changes both monthly and final-payment pressure.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

How does a PCP calculator estimate monthly payment?

It uses financed amount, APR, term, GFV, and option fee to estimate equal monthly installments.

02

What is GFV in a PCP agreement?

GFV is the deferred value due at the end if you choose to purchase the vehicle.

03

How does option fee affect final payment?

Final payment equals GFV plus option fee, so a higher fee raises the amount due at term end.

04

Can deposit be entered as amount or percent?

Yes. Use the deposit toggle to switch between absolute amount and percentage input.

05

Can term be switched between years and months?

Yes. The term unit toggle converts values between years and months without recalculating until Calculate.

06

Does editing sliders recalculate immediately?

No. Results and explanation values update only after clicking Calculate PCP.

07

Why are PCP monthly payments usually lower?

Part of the principal is deferred to the final payment, so less principal is repaid during monthly installments.

08

What does total payable include in PCP?

Total payable includes deposit, all monthly payments, and the final payment including option fee.

09

Can GFV and option fee exceed financed amount?

No. Final payment is constrained so it cannot exceed the financed balance.

10

Are these PCP results lender-specific quotations?

No. These are fixed-rate estimates and can differ from lender offers and contract terms.
