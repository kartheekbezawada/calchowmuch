How to Guide

Use this route when you want to compare the buying power of one amount across two historical U.S. CPI months. It works well for salary comparisons, budget benchmarks, old contract values, and any “what is that worth now?” question tied to inflation.

  1. Enter the amount you want to restate.
  2. Choose the earlier CPI month as the starting point.
  3. Choose the later CPI month as the target point.
  4. Click Calculate to see the equivalent value, CPI factor, and inflation rates.

The core comparison uses a simple ratio: Equivalent Value = Amount × (End CPI ÷ Start CPI). If the end CPI is higher than the start CPI, the same basket of goods costs more in the target month.

FAQ

What does this inflation calculator do?

It converts an amount from one U.S. CPI month into the equivalent value in a later CPI month so you can compare buying power directly.

What CPI series does it use?

It uses the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics CPI-U series `CUUR0000SA0` bundled locally with the route.

Why is the end amount usually higher than the start amount?

Because inflation usually raises the CPI level over time, so more dollars are needed in the later month to match the earlier month’s buying power.

What is cumulative inflation?

It is the full percentage change in CPI between the selected start month and end month.

What is annualized inflation?

It is the average yearly inflation pace implied by the full-period CPI change between the two selected months.

Can I compare a month to itself?

Yes. If the same month is selected for both inputs, the equivalent amount stays the same and inflation is zero.

Why can’t I choose a later start month than end month?

This route is scoped to earlier-to-later historical comparisons so the inflation direction stays consistent and the explanation remains clear.

Why might a month be unavailable?

If the official BLS series does not publish a month value, the calculator blocks that month instead of filling the gap with an estimate.

Is this the same as my personal inflation rate?

No. CPI-U measures broad consumer prices, but your personal spending mix may rise faster or slower than the national index.

Can I use this for contracts, salaries, or budgets?

Yes. It is useful for restating older dollar amounts into later-dollar terms when you want a quick CPI-based purchasing-power comparison.

Important Notes

  • Last updated: March 2026.
  • Accuracy: Uses locally bundled U.S. CPI-U values from the Bureau of Labor Statistics series `CUUR0000SA0`.
  • Disclaimer: This is a CPI-based purchasing-power estimate. It is not an investment return, wage-growth, or personal-cost-of-living forecast.
  • Assumptions: The calculator compares one published CPI month with a later published CPI month. If the official series has a missing month, the route rejects that month instead of interpolating it.
  • Privacy: All calculations run locally in your browser - no data is stored.
