What You Learn at a Glance

This birthday day-of-week calculator is built like a planning surface, not a plain answer box. It tells you the weekday you were born on, how your birthday lands in a selected year, when the next birthday arrives, and which future birthdays are best positioned for long weekends.

For the current selection, your birthday date was on its birth weekday. In the selected year, it lands on that year's weekday. Your next birthday is still to be calculated.

How to Use the Product

Enter your date of birth, keep the default target year or tap one of the quick year shortcuts, then select Calculate. The top result area becomes your birthday snapshot, while the lower sections turn into a planning view for future celebrations.

The target year is useful when you want to answer specific planning questions such as whether a birthday lands on a weekday, a weekend, or close to travel-friendly dates.

Why the Weekday Changes Over Time

A normal year has 365 days, which is 52 full weeks plus one extra day. That extra day shifts a repeating date like a birthday forward by one weekday each year. Leap years add an extra day in February, which creates a bigger shift for birthdays after February 28.

This is why a birthday can move from Friday to Sunday over the years and why weekend birthdays appear in repeating but uneven patterns.

How the Next Birthday Snapshot Helps

The next birthday card turns a curiosity tool into a planning tool. It shows the next occurrence date, the weekday, the age reached on that date, and how many days remain. That is the information people usually need when deciding whether to host an event, book a trip, or set reminders.

The weekend radar below it extends that idea further by surfacing the next Friday, Saturday, and Sunday birthday opportunities in the 12-year map.

Leap-Day Birthdays

If you were born on February 29, the calculator keeps February 29 in leap years. In non-leap target years and non-leap next-birthday calculations, it maps the recurring birthday to February 28 so the preview stays usable for planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the birth weekday accurate?

Yes. The weekday is calculated using standard Gregorian calendar rules for the date entered.

Why does the same birthday move to different weekdays?

Because calendar years are not made of an exact number of full weeks. The leftover day or leap day shifts the weekday each year.

Can I use this to plan future birthday weekends?

Yes. The 12-year recurrence map and weekend radar are designed specifically to help you spot better Friday, Saturday, and Sunday birthday years.

What happens for February 29 birthdays?

Leap-day birthdays stay on February 29 in leap years and use February 28 in non-leap preview years.

Does this store my birthday?

No. The calculator runs in your browser and does not save the birthday you enter.

How far forward can I preview?

You can preview target years from 1600 to 2100, which covers the supported range of this calculator.
