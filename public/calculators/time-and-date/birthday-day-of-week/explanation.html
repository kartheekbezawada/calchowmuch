How to use it

Enter your date of birth, keep the default year or tap a quick year, then calculate. Use the top result for the instant answer and open the look-ahead section only when you want to plan a future birthday.

Why the weekday moves

A normal year is 365 days, which is 52 full weeks plus one extra day. That leftover day shifts a repeating date like a birthday forward by one weekday. Leap years add an extra day in February, which creates a larger shift for birthdays after February 28.

Leap-day birthdays

If you were born on February 29, the calculator keeps February 29 in leap years and uses February 28 in non-leap preview years and non-leap next-birthday calculations so the result stays practical for planning.

FAQ and privacy

Is the birth weekday accurate? Yes. The result uses standard Gregorian calendar rules across the supported date range.

Can I use this to plan future birthday weekends? Yes. That is exactly what the weekend radar and 12-year recurrence map are for.

How far forward can I preview? The supported target year range is 1600 to 2100.

Does this store my birthday? No. Your date stays in the browser and is not submitted anywhere.
