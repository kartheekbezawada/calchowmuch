Birthdays move because a calendar year is not an exact number of weeks. A normal year adds one leftover day, and a leap year adds an extra day in February. That is why the same date rotates through different weekdays over time.

February 29 birthdays stay on February 29 in leap years. In non-leap preview years and non-leap next-birthday calculations, the planner uses February 28 so the answer stays practical for real-world planning.

FAQ

Is the birth weekday accurate?

Yes. The result follows standard Gregorian calendar rules across the supported range.

Why does the weekday change every year?

Because a normal year leaves one extra day after complete weeks, and leap years can shift it again.

Can I plan future birthday weekends here?

Yes. Use the Weekend insights section and the 12-year map to find Friday, Saturday, or Sunday birthdays quickly.

What happens for February 29 birthdays?

Leap-day birthdays stay on February 29 in leap years and use February 28 in non-leap preview years.

How far forward can I preview?

The supported target-year range is 1600 to 2100.

Does this store my birthday?

No. The planner runs in the browser and does not submit your birthday anywhere.
