When Should You Wake Up From a Nap?

Use this nap time calculator to choose a wake-up time based on when you start your nap and the type of nap you want. If you start at {START_TIME} and choose {NAP_TYPE}, it suggests waking up at {WAKE_TIME}.

  • Nap duration is selected from the chosen nap type.
  • A wake buffer is added on top of the nap duration.
  • Total wake target = start time + nap duration + wake buffer.

Quick Nap (10–20 minutes)

  • Fast boost in alertness.
  • Easy to fit into a busy day.
  • Lower chance of waking up groggy.

Power Nap (25–30 minutes)

  • More restorative than a very short nap.
  • Often improves focus and mood.
  • Can still feel groggy for some people if timing is off.

Afternoon Nap (60–90 minutes)

  • Can provide deeper recovery when very tired.
  • Higher chance of sleep inertia after waking.
  • More likely to affect nighttime sleep if taken too late.

A wake buffer adds extra minutes after nap duration so you can wake up gradually. This helps with transition time before your next task.

  • 0 min: strict wake-up target.
  • 5 min: balanced option for most users.
  • 10 min: extra transition time.
  • Uses fixed nap durations for simplicity.
  • Uses your local browser time for “now”.
  • Provides general estimates, not medical advice.
  • Results can vary from person to person.

Which nap is best for work breaks?

Quick naps and power naps are usually the easiest to fit into a workday because they are shorter.

Why do I sometimes feel worse after a nap?

If you wake up during deeper sleep, you may feel groggy for a while. A shorter nap or a different nap length can help.

Will napping ruin my sleep at night?

Naps taken too late in the day can make it harder to fall asleep at night. If this happens, try a shorter nap or nap earlier.

What is the best time of day to nap?

Many people prefer early afternoon. If you nap late, it may affect nighttime sleep.

Does the calculator set an alarm?

No. It only suggests a wake-up time. You can set an alarm on your phone or device.

Important Notes

  • Nap lengths in this calculator are fixed presets for simplicity.
  • The wake buffer gives you extra time to come around after the nap.
  • Individual sleep inertia varies, so exact results can feel different from person to person.
  • Use this as a planning aid, not as medical advice.
