Age planning

How old am I on a specific date?

This age calculator shows exact age in years, months, and days for any birth date and any as-of date. It also summarizes total months, weeks, and days, then shows the next birthday countdown so you can answer the practical question first without manual calendar math.

Use it when you need age today, age on a future event date, or age on a past reference date. The result stays date-based and calendar-aware, which keeps it aligned with how age is usually stated in planning, school, benefits, and milestone scenarios.

How to Guide

Use the calculator in three short steps, then read the exact age first.

  1. Enter the date of birth.
  2. Keep the as-of date on today or choose any other calendar date.
  3. Click Calculate age to refresh the answer cards and supporting totals.

The main answer gives the calendar age first, while the supporting cards show the birth weekday, next birthday, and total-duration views that help with planning.

FAQ

How do I calculate exact age in years, months, and days?

Enter a birth date and an as-of date. The calculator counts full years, then full months, then the remaining days.

Can I calculate age on a past or future date?

Yes. Change the as-of date to any calendar date and calculate again.

Does this age calculator handle leap years correctly?

Yes. Leap years and real month lengths are handled with calendar-aware rules.

What happens for a February 29 birthday in a non-leap year?

For the next-birthday panel, the route standard is to use February 28 when the target year is not a leap year.

Why do total days and exact age look different?

Exact age is a calendar breakdown. Total days is one continuous day count from birth to the selected as-of date.

What does the next birthday section show?

It shows how many days remain, what weekday the birthday lands on, and the age reached on that date.

Can I use this to compare the age of two people?

Yes. Use one date of birth as the birth date and the other as the as-of date to get the age difference.

Does the calculator use time zones or time of birth?

No. It works with calendar dates only, which matches how age is usually stated.

Is this suitable for official age verification?

No. It is useful for planning and reference, but legal proof still comes from documents.

What if the as-of date is before the birth date?

That is the one blocking error. Birth date must be on or before the as-of date.
