Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is simple interest?

Simple interest is interest calculated only on the original principal amount — it never compounds on previously earned interest.

02

What is the simple interest formula?

I = P × r × t, where P is principal, r is the rate as a decimal, and t is time in years (or months if using per-month basis).

03

What is the difference between simple and compound interest?

Simple interest applies only to the principal, growing linearly. Compound interest earns interest on both principal and accumulated interest, growing exponentially.

04

Can I use this for loans?

Yes. Many short-term loans, auto loans, and personal loans use simple interest. This calculator estimates the total interest owed.

05

Does simple interest grow linearly?

Yes. With a fixed rate the interest earned each period is the same, producing a straight-line growth pattern.

06

How do I calculate interest for months?

Set the time unit to "Months." The calculator converts months to years by dividing by 12, then applies the standard formula.

07

What happens if the rate is 0%?

Total interest is zero and the ending amount equals the principal — your money neither grows nor shrinks.

08

What is Interest Basis?

"Per Year" treats the entered rate as an annual rate. "Per Month" treats it as a monthly rate, which dramatically changes the result over longer periods.

09

Is simple interest used for savings?

Rarely for long-term savings — most savings accounts compound. But simple interest is useful for quick comparisons and short-term estimates.

10

When should I use simple interest?

Use it for short-term loans, quick "back of the envelope" estimates, certificates of deposit with simple interest, and educational comparisons.
