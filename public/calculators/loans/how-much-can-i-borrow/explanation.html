How lenders assess borrowing

Lenders use a mix of income multiples and affordability checks to estimate how much you can comfortably repay each month. They also stress test applications at higher interest rates to ensure your budget could handle future rate increases.

When your income, expenses, debts, interest rate, or loan term change, your estimated borrowing can change too. Different lenders apply different rules, so the amount you are approved for may be higher or lower than this estimate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a guaranteed amount I can borrow?

No. This is only an estimate. Final approval depends on your credit score, employment stability, and the lender's policies.

Why is my borrowing less than 5x my income?

Because lenders adjust for your expenses, existing debts, and interest rate stress tests.

Can I borrow more with a longer loan term?

Often yes - a longer term can lower monthly payments and increase affordability, but you will pay more interest overall.

Do different lenders give different amounts?

Yes. Some lenders are more flexible with expenses or income multiples than others.

Should I speak to a broker?

If you are serious about buying, a broker can match you with the lender most likely to approve your application.
