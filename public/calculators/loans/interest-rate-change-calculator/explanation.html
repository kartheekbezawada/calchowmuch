Frequently Asked Questions

01

What does this calculator compare?

It compares baseline mortgage payments and interest against a new rate scenario over your remaining term.

02

Why can small rate moves matter so much?

Mortgage balances are large and terms are long, so even small rate differences can compound into meaningful payment and interest changes.

03

What does Apply Immediately mean?

It assumes the new rate starts from month one of your remaining term, so all projected payments use the updated rate.

04

What does After X Months mean?

It keeps the current rate for the selected months, then recalculates payment for the remaining term at the new rate.

05

Does this include taxes and insurance?

No. This tool focuses on principal and interest so you can isolate the effect of rate changes on core mortgage cost.

06

Can a lower rate reduce total interest?

Yes. If all else is unchanged, a lower rate generally reduces both monthly payment and total interest paid.

07

Can a higher rate increase total interest?

Yes. Higher rates typically raise monthly payment and can significantly increase lifetime interest over the remaining term.

08

Why view both monthly and yearly tables?

Monthly view helps with timing and budget detail, while yearly view makes long-term trend comparison easier.

09

Is this an official lender quote?

No. It is a planning estimate. Final lender terms may differ due to product rules, fees, and underwriting.

10

How should I use this to make decisions?

Run multiple new-rate and timing scenarios, then compare monthly affordability and lifetime interest direction before choosing a strategy.
