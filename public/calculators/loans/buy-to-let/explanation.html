Frequently Asked Questions

What is a buy-to-let mortgage?

A buy-to-let mortgage is a loan designed for purchasing property to rent out to tenants rather than for personal occupation. These mortgages typically require larger deposits (usually 25% or more) and have different affordability criteria based on expected rental income rather than just personal income.

How does a buy-to-let mortgage work?

You borrow money to purchase a rental property, make monthly mortgage payments, and collect rent from tenants. The rental income should ideally cover the mortgage payment plus additional costs (maintenance, insurance, letting fees, void periods). Any surplus is your profit or cashflow.

What deposit do I need for a buy-to-let mortgage?

Most lenders require a minimum deposit of 25% of the property value for buy-to-let mortgages. Some lenders may accept 20%, but larger deposits (30-40%) often secure better interest rates and loan terms.

Are buy-to-let interest rates higher than residential mortgages?

Yes, buy-to-let mortgage rates are typically higher than standard residential mortgages because lenders consider them higher risk. The difference is usually between 0.5% to 1.5% higher than equivalent residential rates.

What is the rental coverage rule?

Most lenders require that the monthly rental income covers 125% to 145% of the monthly mortgage payment (calculated on an interest-only basis). This buffer ensures you can still afford the mortgage during void periods or if interest rates rise.

Can first-time buyers get a buy-to-let mortgage?

While possible, it's more difficult. Most lenders prefer borrowers who already own their own home or have previous landlord experience. First-time landlords may face higher rates, larger deposit requirements, or need to demonstrate significant rental income coverage.

Can I live in my buy-to-let property?

No, buy-to-let mortgages are specifically for rental properties. Living in the property would breach your mortgage terms. If you want to live in the property, you need a residential mortgage instead.

Do I need a separate income for a buy-to-let mortgage?

Many lenders require you to have a minimum personal income (often £25,000-£30,000 per year) in addition to the expected rental income. This demonstrates you can cover mortgage payments during void periods without relying solely on rent.

What mortgage terms are available for buy-to-let?

Buy-to-let mortgages typically range from 5 to 35 years, though some lenders impose age limits (e.g., the mortgage must be repaid by age 70 or 75). Shorter terms mean higher monthly payments but less interest paid overall.

Should I choose interest-only or repayment?

Interest-only mortgages are popular for buy-to-let because they maximize monthly cashflow. You only pay the interest each month, not the capital, which keeps payments lower. However, you'll need a repayment strategy for the capital at the end of the term (usually selling the property). Repayment mortgages build equity faster but reduce monthly cashflow.
