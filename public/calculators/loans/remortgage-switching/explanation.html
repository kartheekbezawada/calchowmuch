Frequently Asked Questions

01

What does this remortgage comparison show?

It compares cumulative cost of your current mortgage versus a new rate and term over your chosen horizon.

02

Why is horizon length important?

A longer horizon shows how differences compound over time, which can change your switching decision.

03

Can a lower rate still be a weak switch?

Yes. If the term changes materially, short-term monthly relief may not always mean strongest long-term value.

04

How should I read break-even month?

It is the first month where cumulative new-deal cost becomes lower than the current-deal cost.

05

Should I compare monthly and yearly table views?

Yes. Monthly reveals timing details, while yearly helps you evaluate strategy with less noise.

06

What if my current payment differs from formula output?

This calculator computes baseline from balance, rate, and term for consistency across all scenarios.

07

Can I use this for quick refinancing checks?

Yes. It is designed for fast directional decisions before lender-specific fee and product validation.

08

Does changing term length affect total saving?

Absolutely. Term length influences monthly payment profile and cumulative interest over your horizon.

09

Is this a lender approval or offer?

No. It is an estimate tool. Final terms depend on product rules, credit profile, and underwriting.

10

How do I use this for better decisions?

Keep balance fixed, vary one input at a time, and compare outcomes across multiple horizon years.
