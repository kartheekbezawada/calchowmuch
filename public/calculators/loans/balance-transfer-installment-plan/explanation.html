Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a credit card balance transfer?

A: A balance transfer moves existing card debt to another card, usually to get a lower APR or promotional APR period.

Q: How is the transfer fee applied?

A: The transfer fee is typically charged once as a percentage of the transferred balance and is added to your starting balance.

Q: Why does promo APR matter so much?

A: A lower promo APR reduces interest during the promo window, which means more of each payment goes to principal.

Q: What happens after promo months end?

A: Any remaining balance starts accruing interest at the post-promo APR, which can increase the total payoff cost.

Q: How do I know if a transfer is worth it?

A: Compare total paid (interest + fee + principal) with and without transfer offers; a transfer is generally better when fee cost is lower than interest savings.

Q: Can I pay off before promo expires?

A: Divide the starting balance (including fee) by promo months and compare that number to your monthly payment.

Q: Does this include new card purchases?

A: No. This scenario assumes no new purchases and focuses only on repaying the transferred balance.

Q: Why can payoff months differ from rough estimates?

A: Monthly interest compounding, fee-loaded starting balance, and post-promo APR timing all affect the final payoff month count.

Q: Can I compare multiple offers with this?

A: Yes. Run each offer with its own fee, promo APR, promo months, and post-promo APR, then compare total paid and payoff time.

Q: What assumptions does this calculator use?

A: It assumes fixed APR inputs, consistent monthly payment, on-time payments, and no additional fees beyond the transfer fee.
