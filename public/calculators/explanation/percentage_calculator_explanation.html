Use these steps to understand how each percentage result is calculated.

  1. What is X% of Y?
    Step 1: Convert the percentage to a decimal by dividing by 100.
    Step 2: Multiply that decimal by the whole amount.
    Example: 10% of 100 → (10 ÷ 100) × 100 = 0.1 × 100 = 10.
  2. X is what percent of Y?
    Step 1: Divide the part (X) by the whole (Y).
    Step 2: Multiply the result by 100 to convert to a percentage.
    Example: 10 is what percent of 100 → (10 ÷ 100) × 100 = 10%.
  3. Percentage increase/decrease from A to B
    Step 1: Find the change: B − A.
    Step 2: Divide the change by the original value (A).
    Step 3: Multiply by 100 to get the percent change.
    Example: 100 to 110 → (110 − 100) ÷ 100 × 100 = 10%.
