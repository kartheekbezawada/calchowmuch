What This Calculator Is Designed to Estimate

A balance transfer calculator estimates whether moving existing debt to a new card with a promotional APR can reduce overall interest and improve payoff timing after considering fee, promo window, and post-promo rate.

The model combines all key variables so you can evaluate whether the transfer likely creates real savings or only short-term payment relief.

Calculate the Transfer Fee and Starting Balance

Transfer fee is generally charged as a percentage of the moved balance and added immediately to the new account. That means repayment starts above the original transferred amount.

How Interest Works Without a Balance Transfer

Without a transfer, debt continues compounding at the existing APR from month one. Monthly interest is charged on remaining principal, so slow principal reduction usually increases total cost over time.

A useful planning check is to compare your monthly payment with the minimum monthly pace needed to clear the starting balance before promo expiry.

When a Balance Transfer Usually Makes Sense

Transfer strategy is generally stronger when promo APR is materially lower than current card APR, transfer fee is manageable, and repayment pace is sufficient to reduce most balance before post-promo rate starts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a credit card balance transfer?

A: A balance transfer moves your existing credit card debt from one card to another, typically to take advantage of a lower interest rate or a promotional 0% APR period.

Q: How does a balance transfer fee work?

A: Most balance transfer cards charge a one-time fee, usually 3-5% of the transferred amount, and this fee is added to the new card balance immediately.

Q: What is a promotional APR period?

A: A promotional APR period is a limited time where transferred debt accrues at a reduced APR, often 0%, so more of each payment goes to principal.

Q: What happens after the promo APR expires?

A: Any remaining balance starts accruing interest at the regular post-promo APR, which can increase monthly interest and extend payoff time.

Q: Is a balance transfer worth the fee?

A: It is usually worth it when interest savings during the promo period are greater than the transfer fee and any post-promo interest on the leftover balance.

Q: How do I calculate if I can pay off before the promo ends?

A: Divide your starting balance (including fee) by promo months. If that value is at or below your monthly payment, you can likely finish before the promo expires.

Q: Can I transfer a balance between cards from the same issuer?

A: Most issuers do not allow transfers between their own cards. Transfers usually must go to a card issued by a different provider.

Q: Does this calculator account for new purchases on the transfer card?

A: No. It models repayment of the transferred balance only and assumes no new purchases are added during payoff.

Q: What APR applies to the remaining balance after the promo period?

A: The post-promo APR applies to any remaining balance once the promotional period ends.

Q: How do I compare multiple balance transfer offers?

A: Run each offer with its own fee, promo APR, promo months, and post-promo APR, then compare payoff months and total paid to identify the lower-cost option.
