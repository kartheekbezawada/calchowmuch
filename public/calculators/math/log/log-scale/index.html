Answer-first

All three modes use positive values. Use the reference fields when you want to compare your measurement against a different baseline.

Decibels compare the measured amplitude against a reference using a base-10 logarithm.

pH uses the negative base-10 logarithm of hydrogen ion concentration, so smaller concentrations produce higher pH values.

Richter magnitude measures how many powers of 10 separate the recorded ground motion from the reference motion.
