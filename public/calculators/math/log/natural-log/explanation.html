Formula Guide

The natural logarithm answers the question, what power of e gives x? That means y = ln(x) is equivalent to e^y = x.

Because e^x and ln(x) are inverse functions, the route also shows the exponential check directly in the answer card. That makes it easy to see whether the rounded ln result still maps back to the original x value.

FAQ

What is a natural logarithm?

It is the logarithm with base e, where e is about 2.718281828.

Why does ln(e) equal 1?

Because e raised to the power of 1 is exactly e.

Why does ln(1) equal 0?

Any positive base raised to the power of 0 equals 1.

Can x be negative?

No. For real-number output, natural logs only accept positive inputs.

Why is ln(x) negative for values below 1?

Because e must be raised to a negative power to produce numbers between 0 and 1.

Why does the graph rise slowly?

The natural log keeps increasing, but it grows much more slowly than a straight line.

What does the exponential check mean?

It shows the inverse relationship by rewriting ln(x) as e raised to the solved value.

Should I increase decimal places?

Use more decimals when you need a tighter numeric check or a more exact rounded answer.

Is this the same as log base 10?

No. Natural logs use base e, while common logs use base 10.

When is ln(x) useful?

It is useful in growth models, continuous compounding, decay problems, and algebraic solving.
