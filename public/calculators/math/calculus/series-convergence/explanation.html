Formula Guide

The ratio test estimates L = lim |a_(n+1) / a_n|. The root test estimates L = lim n-th root of |a_n|. The comparison test estimates whether the term behaves like a familiar 1 / n^p pattern.

A limit below 1 suggests convergence for ratio and root tests, while a limit above 1 suggests divergence. A value near 1 usually means that test alone is inconclusive.

FAQ

What does convergence mean for a series?

It means the partial sums approach a finite value instead of growing without bound or oscillating forever.

Why can one test be inconclusive?

Some tests only give a clear answer when the limit is strictly below or above 1, so a borderline value needs another method.

When should I use the ratio test?

It is especially useful for factorials, exponentials, and expressions where successive terms simplify neatly.

When is the root test better?

It is useful when the term already contains an n-th power or when the ratio is awkward to simplify.

What is a p-series?

It is a family of series of the form 1 / n^p, which converges for p greater than 1 and diverges otherwise.

Does the route prove convergence for every possible series?

No. It is a practical calculator for common series forms, not a full symbolic analysis system.
