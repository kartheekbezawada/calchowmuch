Quick Answer Table

Quick answer table for the fraction calculator
Mode Rule What to watch
Add Find a common denominator, then add the numerators. Do not add the denominators together.
Subtract Find a common denominator, then subtract the numerators. Keep the same-size parts before subtracting.
Multiply Multiply top by top and bottom by bottom. No common denominator is needed first.
Divide Keep the first fraction, flip the second, then multiply. Only the second fraction is flipped.
Simplify Divide the numerator and denominator by the greatest common divisor. Use the same factor on both parts.
Convert Use division for mixed numbers and whole x denominator + numerator for improper fractions. Keep the denominator in the fractional part of the mixed number.

Formula Notes

For addition and subtraction, the calculator uses a/b +/- c/d = (ad +/- bc) / bd and then simplifies the result. For multiplication, it uses a/b x c/d = ac / bd. For division, it uses a/b / c/d = a/b x d/c.

Simplification uses the greatest common divisor, and conversion uses quotient and remainder rules to move between improper fractions and mixed numbers.

FAQ

What is a fraction?

A fraction shows part of a whole with a numerator on top and a denominator on the bottom.

Why do denominators need to match when adding fractions?

You can only combine parts that are measured in the same size pieces.

Do you need a common denominator when multiplying fractions?

No. Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together, then simplify if possible.

What does reciprocal mean in fraction division?

It means flipping a fraction so the numerator and denominator change places.

What is the simplest form of a fraction?

It is the version where the numerator and denominator share no common factor greater than 1.

What is an improper fraction?

It is a fraction where the numerator is equal to or larger than the denominator.

What is a mixed number?

It is a whole number written together with a proper fraction.

Can fractions be negative?

Yes. A negative fraction means the value is below zero.

Why can a denominator not be zero?

A valid fraction cannot split a whole into zero equal parts, so the denominator must stay non-zero.

Should I simplify every final answer?

Usually yes, unless a teacher or worksheet asks for a different form.
