Add Fractions

To add fractions, find a common denominator (typically the least common multiple), convert both fractions, add the numerators, and simplify.

Example: 1/4 + 1/8

  • Common denominator: 8
  • Convert: 1/4 = 2/8
  • Add: 2/8 + 1/8 = 3/8

Subtract Fractions

Subtraction works the same as addition, but you subtract the numerators instead.

Example: 3/4 ƒ^' 1/6

  • Common denominator: 12
  • Convert: 3/4 = 9/12, 1/6 = 2/12
  • Subtract: 9/12 ƒ^' 2/12 = 7/12

Multiply Fractions

Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together, then simplify.

Example: 2/3 ا- 3/5

  • Multiply numerators: 2 ا- 3 = 6
  • Multiply denominators: 3 ا- 5 = 15
  • Result: 6/15 = 2/5 (simplified)

Divide Fractions

To divide fractions, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal (flipped version) of the second.

Example: 2/3 اْ 4/5

  • Reciprocal of 4/5: 5/4
  • Multiply: 2/3 ا- 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6 (simplified)

Simplify Fraction

Simplifying a fraction means dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor (GCD).

Example: 6/8

  • GCD of 6 and 8: 2
  • Divide both: 6اْ2 = 3, 8اْ2 = 4
  • Simplified: 3/4

An improper fraction has a numerator larger than the denominator. A mixed number combines a whole number and a fraction.
