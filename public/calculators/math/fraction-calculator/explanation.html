Complete Practical Guide

A fraction has a numerator on top and a denominator on the bottom. The denominator tells you the size of the parts. Addition and subtraction need matching denominators first. Multiplication goes straight across. Division flips the second fraction first. Simplifying uses the greatest common divisor. Converting uses quotient and remainder or whole times denominator plus numerator.

The fastest way to learn with this page is to keep the default example once, study the steps, and then replace the numbers with your own problem.

Frequently Asked Questions

10 answers
01What is a fraction?

A fraction shows part of a whole using a numerator on top and a denominator on the bottom.

02Why do denominators need to match when adding fractions?

Because you can only combine parts that are measured in the same size pieces.

03Do you need a common denominator when multiplying fractions?

No. You multiply the numerators together and the denominators together.

04What does reciprocal mean in fraction division?

It means flipping a fraction so the numerator and denominator swap places.

05What is the simplest form of a fraction?

It is the version where the numerator and denominator share no factor greater than 1.

06What is an improper fraction?

It is a fraction where the numerator is equal to or larger than the denominator.

07What is a mixed number?

It is a number made from a whole number and a proper fraction together.

08Can fractions be negative?

Yes. A negative fraction means the value is below zero.

09Why can a denominator not be zero?

Because a whole cannot be divided into zero equal parts in a valid fraction.

10Should I simplify every final answer?

Yes, unless your teacher asks for a different form, simplest form is usually the expected final answer.

Important Notes

  • Always check that the denominator is not zero before you start.
  • For addition and subtraction, focus on matching denominators before combining numerators.
  • For division, flip only the second fraction, not the first one.
  • Simplifying changes the look of the fraction but not its value.
  • Use the worked steps to understand the method, not just to copy the answer.
  • This calculator does not store your numbers after you leave the page.
