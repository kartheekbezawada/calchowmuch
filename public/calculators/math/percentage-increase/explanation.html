Percent Change

This shows how much the ending value changes compared to the starting value. We turn the difference into a percent of the starting value.

  1. Difference = 260 - 200 = 60
  2. Ratio = Difference / Starting value = 60 / 200 = 0.3
  3. Percent change = Ratio x 100 = 30%

Output: 30% change with a difference of 60.

Percent Of a Value

This calculates a percentage of a value by turning the percent into a decimal, then multiplying by the value.

  1. Decimal form of the percent = 15% / 100 = 0.15
  2. Multiply the decimal by the value = 0.15 x 400 = 60

Output: 15% of 400 is 60.

Increase By a Percent

This finds the increase amount and then adds it to the original value.

  1. Increase amount = 150 x 20% = 30
  2. New value = 150 + 30 = 180

Output: 150 increased by 20% is 180.

Decrease By a Percent

This finds the decrease amount and then subtracts it from the original value.

  1. Decrease amount = 150 x 20% = 30
  2. New value = 150 - 30 = 120

Output: 150 decreased by 20% is 120.

What Percent One Value Is of Another

This finds the ratio of part to total and converts it to a percent.

  1. Ratio = Part / Total = 45 / 180 = 0.25
  2. Percent = Ratio x 100 = 25%

Output: 45 is 25% of 180.
