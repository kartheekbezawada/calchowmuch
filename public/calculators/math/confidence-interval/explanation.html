Confidence Interval Calculator Practical Guide

Use this calculator to estimate a confidence interval around a sample proportion or sample mean in one answer-first result. It keeps the lower bound, upper bound, margin of error, and formula summary together so you can assess the interval quickly.

  • Best for: quick interval estimates on sample proportions or known-sigma means.
  • Quick answer flow: choose the mode, set the confidence level, enter the sample values, and calculate once.
  • Output includes: interval bounds, standard error, margin of error, and formula summary.

How to Guide

  1. Select Proportion for p-hat or Mean for x-bar with known sigma.
  2. Pick 90%, 95%, or 99% confidence.
  3. Enter the sample values and click Calculate confidence interval.
  4. Read the interval bounds first, then the error and formula notes underneath.

Quick Answer Table

Quick answer table for the confidence interval calculator

Proportion Sample proportion p-hat, sample size, and z-value Useful for yes/no or success-rate style samples.
Mean Sample mean, known sigma, sample size, and z-value Useful when you already know the population standard deviation.
Confidence level The selected z-value Higher confidence gives a wider interval.
Margin of error The distance from the centre to each bound A smaller margin means a tighter interval.

Formula Guide

For proportions, the route uses p-hat ± z × sqrt(p(1-p)/n). For means with known sigma, it uses x-bar ± z × sigma / sqrt(n). In both cases, a larger sample size narrows the interval, while a higher confidence level widens it.

FAQ

What does a confidence interval mean?

It gives a plausible range for the population value based on your sample and chosen confidence level.

Why does higher confidence make the interval wider?

Because the route uses a larger z-value, which increases the margin of error.

When should I use proportion mode?

Use proportion mode when the sample result is a percent or share, such as approval rate or conversion rate.

When should I use mean mode?

Use mean mode when you are estimating an average and you already know the population standard deviation.

What narrows the interval?

A larger sample size or a lower confidence level narrows the interval.

Can the proportion interval go below 0% or above 100%?

The route keeps the result inside valid proportion bounds.
