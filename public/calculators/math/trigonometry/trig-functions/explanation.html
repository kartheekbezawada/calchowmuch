Six Trigonometric Functions

The core functions are sin, cos, and tan. Their reciprocals are sec, csc, and cot. These functions model waves, rotations, and periodic motion.

Exact Values

Special angles like 30, 45, and 60 degrees have exact values. The calculator shows both the exact forms (like sqrt(3)/2) and decimal approximations.

Key Identities

  • sin^2(x) + cos^2(x) = 1
  • tan(x) = sin(x) / cos(x)
  • sec(x) = 1 / cos(x), csc(x) = 1 / sin(x), cot(x) = cos(x) / sin(x)

Graph Interpretation

Amplitude controls the height of the wave, while period stretches the horizontal length of one cycle. For tan, sec, csc, and cot, vertical asymptotes appear where the functions are undefined.

Applications

Trig functions appear in electrical engineering, sound synthesis, structural analysis, and orbital mechanics. Adjusting amplitude and period mirrors real-world parameters.
