Law of Sines and Cosines Practical Guide

Use this Law of Sines and Cosines calculator to solve triangle sides, angles, and area from the values you already know. The route keeps the answer card, recommended law, and diagram together so you can check whether the triangle setup is valid without digging through dense trigonometry notes.

  • Best for: checking SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, and SSA triangle cases quickly.
  • Quick answer flow: enter known values, let auto detect the law, then solve once.
  • Output includes: solved sides, solved angles, area, triangle pattern, and method notes.

How to Guide

  1. Leave blank any side or angle you do not already know.
  2. Choose degrees or radians for angle entry.
  3. Keep Auto Detect on unless you want to force one trig law.
  4. Click Solve with Trig Laws and read the answer card before checking the diagram.

Formula Guide

The Law of Sines links matching sides and angles: a / sin(A) = b / sin(B) = c / sin(C). It is usually the best fit when you know two angles and one side, or one side-angle pair plus another side.

The Law of Cosines works when you know three sides or two sides and the included angle: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). Once the triangle is solved, the route also reports the area so you can sanity-check the result size quickly.

FAQ

When should I use the Law of Sines?

Use it when you know a matching side-angle pair, especially in ASA, AAS, or SSA cases.

When should I use the Law of Cosines?

Use it for SSS and SAS triangles where the included angle or all sides are known.

What does auto detect do?

It reads the values you entered and recommends the trig law that matches that triangle pattern.

Can this route solve SSA triangles?

Yes. It can report one valid triangle, two valid triangles, or no valid triangle for SSA input.

Why can SSA produce two answers?

The missing side can swing into two valid shapes when the known side-angle data is ambiguous.

Does the route show area too?

Yes. The answer card includes area after the route solves the full triangle.

Can I enter radians?

Yes. Switch the angle-unit control to radians before solving.

Do I need to fill every field?

No. Leave unknown values blank and provide a valid three-value combination that includes a side.

What if the triangle is invalid?

The route shows a clear error when the side or angle rules do not form a real triangle.

Is this useful outside classwork?

Yes. It also helps with layout estimates, navigation bearings, and quick surveying checks.
