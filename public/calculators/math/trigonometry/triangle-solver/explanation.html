Triangle Notation

Sides a, b, and c are opposite angles A, B, and C. Angles always sum to 180 degrees. The solver uses this convention to compute the missing values.

Law of Sines

Use the Law of Sines when you know a side-angle pair: a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C). It is best for ASA, AAS, or SSA inputs.

Law of Cosines

Use the Law of Cosines for SAS or SSS: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). This finds the missing side or angle directly.

SSA Ambiguous Case

SSA can create two valid triangles because a given side length can swing to two possible angles. The calculator reports both solutions when they exist.

Practical Uses

Triangle solving is used in surveying, navigation, architecture, and physics when distances or angles are partially known. The diagram helps confirm proportions.
