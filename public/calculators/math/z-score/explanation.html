Z-Score Calculator Practical Guide

Use this calculator to convert one raw value into a z-score and read how far it sits above or below the mean. The answer-first result keeps the standard score, formula, and interpretation together so you can assess the value quickly.

  • Best for: quick standard-score interpretation on one value.
  • Quick answer flow: enter x, mean, and standard deviation, then calculate once.
  • Output includes: z-score, formula substitution, and above/below-mean interpretation.

How to Guide

  1. Enter the raw value, the mean, and the standard deviation.
  2. Click Calculate z-score to update the answer card.
  3. Read the z-score first, then use the interpretation line to see whether the value is above or below the mean.
  4. Use the formula block when you want to verify the substitution manually.

Formula Guide

The route uses z = (x - mu) / sigma. It subtracts the mean from the raw value, then divides the difference by the standard deviation to show how many standard deviations away the value sits.

FAQ

What does a z-score of 0 mean?

It means the value is exactly equal to the mean.

What does a positive z-score mean?

It means the value is above the mean.

What does a negative z-score mean?

It means the value is below the mean.

Why does a larger absolute z-score matter?

Because it shows the value is further from average and may be more unusual.

Can I use decimals?

Yes. The route accepts decimal values for the score, mean, and standard deviation.

What if the standard deviation is zero?

The route blocks that input because dividing by zero would make the z-score invalid.
