Why Factor Polynomials?

Factoring rewrites a polynomial as a product of simpler expressions. It helps solve equations, find roots, and simplify algebraic expressions.

Common Factoring Methods

  • Greatest Common Factor (GCF): Pull out shared factors.
  • Quadratic factoring: Find two binomials that multiply to the quadratic.
  • Difference of squares: a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b).
  • Sum/Difference of cubes: a^3 Â± b^3 formulas.
  • Grouping: Factor pairs of terms and combine.

Worked Example (Quadratic)

Factor x^2 + 5x + 6:

  1. Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5: 2 and 3.
  2. Rewrite: x^2 + 2x + 3x + 6.
  3. Group: x(x + 2) + 3(x + 2).
  4. Factor: (x + 2)(x + 3).

Difference of Squares Example

x^2 - 9 = (x - 3)(x + 3)

Tips

  • Always check for a GCF first.
  • Look for special patterns like squares and cubes.
  • Verify by multiplying your factors back together.
