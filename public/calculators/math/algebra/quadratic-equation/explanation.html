Quadratic Equation Solver Practical Guide

Use this calculator to solve quadratic equations quickly and verify whether the roots are real or complex using the discriminant.

How to Guide

  1. Enter coefficients a, b, and c from your equation.
  2. Click Solve Equation to calculate roots.
  3. Read the root type from the result summary.
  4. Review the step-by-step details for discriminant and substitution.

Important Notes

Last updated: February 2026

  • Coefficient a cannot be zero for quadratic equations.
  • Discriminant > 0 gives two real roots, = 0 gives one repeated root, and < 0 gives complex roots.
  • Complex roots are shown in a ± bi format.

FAQ

What happens when a is zero?

The expression is no longer quadratic, so this solver returns an error.

Why are there two answers for many equations?

Most parabolas cross the x-axis at two points, which creates two roots.

Can this solve decimal coefficients?

Yes, decimal values are supported for all coefficients.

Does it show working steps?

Yes, the details panel includes formula substitution and discriminant analysis.
