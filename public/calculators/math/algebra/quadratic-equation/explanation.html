What Is a Quadratic Equation?

A quadratic equation has the form ax^2 + bx + c = 0, where a is not zero. The graph is a parabola, and the solutions are the x-values where the parabola crosses the x-axis.

Quadratic Formula

Use the quadratic formula to solve for x:

x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / (2a)

The discriminant Δ = b^2 - 4ac determines the type of solutions.

Discriminant Cases

  • Δ > 0: Two distinct real roots.
  • Δ = 0: One repeated real root.
  • Δ < 0: Two complex roots (a ± bi).

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Identify a, b, and c.
  2. Compute the discriminant Δ = b^2 - 4ac.
  3. Plug into the quadratic formula.
  4. Simplify to obtain the roots.

Graph Interpretation

The parabola opens upward when a > 0 and downward when a < 0. The vertex marks the highest or lowest point, and x-intercepts show real solutions when they exist.

Worked Example

For x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0:

  1. a = 1, b = -5, c = 6
  2. Δ = (-5)^2 - 4(1)(6) = 25 - 24 = 1
  3. x = (5 ± √1) / 2 = (5 ± 1) / 2
  4. x = 2 and x = 3
