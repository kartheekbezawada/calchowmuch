Slope and Distance Calculator Practical Guide

Use this calculator to analyze a line segment from two coordinate points, including distance, midpoint, and equation forms.

How to Guide

  1. Enter coordinates for Point 1 and Point 2.
  2. Click Calculate Properties.
  3. Read slope, distance, and midpoint in the summary output.
  4. Review line equations and parallel/perpendicular references in the detail panel.

Important Notes

Last updated: February 2026

  • If x1 equals x2, the slope is undefined and the line is vertical.
  • Distance uses the Euclidean formula sqrt((x2-x1)^2 + (y2-y1)^2).
  • Perpendicular slope is the negative reciprocal when slope is finite and non-zero.

FAQ

Why does slope show undefined?

Undefined slope means both points share the same x-value, so the line is vertical.

Can I enter decimal coordinates?

Yes, decimal values are supported for all coordinates.

What does midpoint represent?

The midpoint is the exact center of the segment connecting the two points.

Are line equations always shown?

Yes. For vertical lines, forms that require finite slope are replaced with vertical-line text.
