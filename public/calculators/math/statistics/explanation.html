What is Descriptive Statistics?

Descriptive statistics summarize and describe the main features of a dataset. This calculator provides a comprehensive summary including measures of central tendency, spread, and variability.

When to Use This Calculator

  • Getting a quick overview of any numeric dataset
  • Summarizing survey or experiment results
  • Comparing datasets using key statistics
  • Academic and research data analysis
  • Business reporting and analytics

Statistics Provided

  • Count (n): The number of values in the dataset.
  • Sum: The total of all values added together.
  • Mean: The arithmetic average (sum divided by count).
  • Median: The middle value when data is sorted.
  • Mode: The most frequently occurring value(s).
  • Minimum: The smallest value.
  • Maximum: The largest value.
  • Range: The difference between max and min.
  • Variance: Average of squared deviations from the mean.
  • Standard Deviation: Square root of variance.

Worked Example

Using the default dataset: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 20, 15, 10, 5

  1. Count: n = 9
  2. Sum: 5 + 10 + 15 + 20 + 25 + 20 + 15 + 10 + 5 = 125
  3. Mean: 125 / 9 = 13.8889
  4. Median: Sorted: 5, 5, 10, 10, 15, 15, 20, 20, 25 → middle = 15
  5. Mode: 5, 10, 15, 20 each appear twice → 5, 10, 15, 20
  6. Min: 5
  7. Max: 25
  8. Range: 25 - 5 = 20

Mean vs. Median vs. Mode

  • Mean: Best for symmetric data without outliers. Affected by extreme values.
  • Median: Better for skewed data. Not affected by outliers.
  • Mode: Useful for categorical data or finding the most common value.

Population vs. Sample Statistics

  • Population: When your data represents the entire group of interest. Uses n as divisor.
  • Sample: When your data is a subset of a larger population. Uses (n-1) as divisor for unbiased estimation.

Mode Interpretation

  • No mode: All values appear with equal frequency (each only once).
  • Single mode (unimodal): One value appears most frequently.
  • Multiple modes (multimodal): Two or more values tie for highest frequency.

Edge Cases and Validation

  • Dataset must contain at least one valid number.
  • Sample variance/std dev requires at least 2 values.
  • Non-numeric values are rejected with an error message.
  • Results are formatted to 4 decimal places.
