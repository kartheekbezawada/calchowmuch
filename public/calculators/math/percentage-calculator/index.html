Explanation

Percent change compares how much a value grows or shrinks relative to its starting point.

  1. Subtract the starting value from the ending value.
  2. Divide that difference by the starting value.
  3. Multiply by 100 to turn it into a percentage.

Example: From 200 to 260 → (260 − 200) ÷ 200 × 100 = 30% increase.

Percentage of a value answers, “How much is X% of Y?”

  1. Convert the percent to a decimal by dividing by 100.
  2. Multiply the decimal by the value.
  3. Interpret the result in the original units.

Example: 25% of 400 → 0.25 × 400 = 100.

This tool finds what percent the part is of the whole.

  1. Divide the part by the whole.
  2. Multiply the result by 100.
  3. Round to a useful number of decimals.

Example: 45 out of 180 → (45 ÷ 180) × 100 = 25%.

Add or subtract a percentage by scaling the starting value.

  1. Convert the percent to a decimal.
  2. Multiply the starting value by 1 + percent for an increase.
  3. Multiply the starting value by 1 − percent for a decrease.

Example: 250 ± 10% → 250 × 1.10 = 275 and 250 × 0.90 = 225.
