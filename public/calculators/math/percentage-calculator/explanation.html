Percentage Increase

Percentage increase shows how much a value has grown relative to its original amount. It's calculated by finding the difference between new and original, dividing by the original, and multiplying by 100.

Example: 80 → 100

  • Increase amount: 100 - 80 = 20
  • Percentage increase: (20 ÷ 80) × 100 = 25%

Percentage Decrease

Percentage decrease measures how much a value has fallen relative to where it started. The calculation is the same as increase, but the result is negative (or shown as a decrease).

Example: 100 → 75

  • Decrease amount: 100 - 75 = 25
  • Percentage decrease: (25 ÷ 100) × 100 = 25%

What % of what

This mode finds what percentage one number (the part) is of another number (the whole). Divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100.

Example: 25 is what % of 200?

  • Calculation: (25 ÷ 200) × 100 = 12.5%

Note: If the whole value is 0, the calculation is undefined (cannot divide by zero).

What is X% of Y

This mode calculates a specific percentage of a given value. Multiply the base value by the percentage and divide by 100.

Example: What is 15% of 80?

  • Calculation: (15 ÷ 100) × 80 = 12
