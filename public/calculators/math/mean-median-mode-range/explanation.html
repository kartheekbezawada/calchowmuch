Mean Median Mode Range Calculator Practical Guide

Use this calculator to turn a raw dataset into the four common classroom summary measures, plus the minimum and maximum, in one answer-first result. It keeps the center and spread outputs together so you can quickly judge where the dataset sits and how wide it runs.

  • Best for: quick descriptive statistics on one list of numbers.
  • Quick answer flow: paste the values, calculate once, and read the answer card first.
  • Output includes: mean, median, mode, minimum, maximum, and range.

How to Guide

  1. Paste the dataset using commas, spaces, or line breaks.
  2. Click Calculate summary to fill the answer card.
  3. Read the mean, median, and mode together before checking the minimum, maximum, and range.
  4. If the list has one repeated value, the mode will highlight it; if not, the route reports no mode.

Range Maximum minus minimum Useful for a quick first look at spread.

Formula Guide

The mean is the sum of all values divided by the count. The median is the middle value in the sorted list, or the average of the two middle values when the count is even. The mode is the most frequent value, and the range is max - min.

Why compare mean and median?

If the mean sits far from the median, the dataset may be skewed by unusually large or small values.

FAQ

When should I use the mean?

Use the mean when you want one average value and the dataset does not have extreme outliers.

When is the median better?

The median is better when one or two extreme values distort the mean.

What if there is no mode?

If every value appears only once, the route reports that there is no mode.

Can there be more than one mode?

Yes. If several values tie for the highest frequency, the route lists them together.

What does the range miss?

Range only looks at the endpoints, so it does not describe how values behave in the middle.

Can I paste values on separate lines?

Yes. Commas, spaces, and line breaks are all accepted.
