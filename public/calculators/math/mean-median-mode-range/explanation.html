What Are Mean, Median, Mode, and Range?

These are the four fundamental measures used to describe a dataset: three measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and one measure of spread (range).

When to Use This Calculator

  • Quick analysis of a small dataset
  • Homework and educational purposes
  • Comparing central tendency measures
  • Understanding data distribution basics
  • Summarizing scores, prices, or measurements

Definitions

  • Mean: The arithmetic average - sum of all values divided by the count.
  • Median: The middle value when data is sorted. For even counts, it's the average of the two middle values.
  • Mode: The value that appears most frequently. A dataset can have no mode, one mode, or multiple modes.
  • Range: The difference between the maximum and minimum values.

Formulas

Mean: Sum of values / Count of values

Median: Middle value (or average of two middle values)

Mode: Most frequent value(s)

Range: Maximum - Minimum

When to Use Each Measure

  • Mean: Best for symmetric distributions without outliers. Most commonly used average.
  • Median: Better for skewed data or when outliers are present. Represents the "typical" value.
  • Mode: Useful for categorical data or finding the most common occurrence.
  • Range: Quick measure of spread, but sensitive to outliers.

Mode Special Cases

  • No mode: When all values appear with equal frequency (each appears once).
  • Unimodal: One value appears more frequently than others.
  • Bimodal: Two values tie for the highest frequency.
  • Multimodal: Three or more values share the highest frequency.

Edge Cases and Validation

  • Dataset must contain at least one valid number.
  • Non-numeric values are rejected with an error message.
  • Single value: mean = median = the value, range = 0, no mode.
  • Results are formatted to 4 decimal places.
