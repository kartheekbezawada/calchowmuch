How To Guide

How much interest can an offset mortgage save me?

An offset mortgage calculator estimates how much interest your linked savings can reduce over the remaining term. It also shows how much earlier you could finish the loan if offset savings are maintained.

In most products, offset savings do not earn separate savings interest. Instead, they reduce the balance that mortgage interest is charged on, so more of each repayment goes to principal.

What this offset mortgage calculator shows

The calculator compares two paths: a baseline mortgage with no offset effect, and an offset path using your savings balance, contribution pattern, and selected mode.

Inputs

  • Mortgage Balance: outstanding mortgage balance.
  • Interest Rate (APR %): annual mortgage rate.
  • Remaining Term (years): years left to repay.
  • Offset Savings Balance: current linked savings.
  • Monthly Offset Contribution: savings added each month.
  • Offset Mode: full offset or partial/average offset.

Outputs

  • Monthly Payment (Baseline): unchanged scheduled repayment.
  • Interest Saved: estimated lifetime interest reduction.
  • Time Saved: earlier payoff versus baseline path.
  • Effective Balance: balance that accrues interest after offset.
  • Baseline vs Offset Interest: total-cost comparison.
  • Amortization Table: monthly/yearly breakdown.

How does an offset mortgage calculator work?

  1. It first computes a baseline payment using standard amortization:

    M = P x [r x (1 + r)^n] / [(1 + r)^n - 1]

  2. It then calculates effective balance each month:

    Full: Effective Balance = Mortgage Balance - Offset Savings

    Partial/Average: Effective Balance = Mortgage Balance - (0.5 x Offset Savings)

  3. Monthly interest in the offset path is charged on that effective balance:

    Offset Interest Charge = Effective Balance x r

  4. Finally, it compares baseline and offset totals:

    Interest Saved = Baseline Total Interest - Offset Total Interest

How to use the offset mortgage calculator

  1. Enter mortgage balance, annual rate, and remaining term.
  2. Enter current offset savings and monthly contribution.
  3. Review Full Offset results.
  4. Switch to Partial / Average for a conservative scenario.
  5. Compare interest saved, payoff time, and yearly trend together.
  6. Run multiple scenarios before final borrowing decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What does an offset account do?

It reduces the balance charged interest by linking savings to your mortgage, which lowers interest while keeping your repayment schedule active.

02

Why does the payment stay the same in this model?

The calculator keeps baseline monthly payment fixed so savings show up as lower interest and faster principal reduction.

03

What is Full Offset mode?

Full mode applies the entire offset savings balance against mortgage balance for interest calculations each month.

04

What is Partial / Average mode?

Partial mode applies 50% of savings to represent daily balance fluctuations in the offset account.

05

How does monthly contribution help?

Regular contribution grows the offset balance over time, increasing the amount shielded from interest and improving savings.

06

What does effective balance mean?

Effective balance is the estimated balance actually accruing interest after offset savings are applied.

07

Why compare monthly and yearly tables?

Monthly view shows precision and trend, while yearly view helps assess strategic savings over a longer horizon.

08

Can offset savings reduce loan term?

Yes. Lower interest means more principal is repaid sooner, which can shorten payoff time.

09

Does this replace lender statements?

No. It is a planning model. Your lender’s compounding rules, product terms, and posting timings may differ.

10

How should I use this for decision-making?

Test several savings balances and contribution levels, then compare interest saved, payoff time reduction, and yearly trend consistency.
