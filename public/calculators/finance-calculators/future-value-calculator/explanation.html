Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is future value?

Future value is the projected amount your money could become after growth over time.

02

How is future value different from present value?

Present value states what money is worth today, while future value projects what it may become later.

03

Why does compounding matter?

Compounding lets growth build on earlier growth, which can accelerate outcomes over longer periods.

04

Should I include recurring contributions?

Yes, when you expect to add a similar amount regularly and want the projection to reflect that habit.

05

Can I use months instead of years?

Yes. Use months when you want a shorter planning horizon or more precise timing.

06

What happens if the rate is 0%?

The ending balance is just the starting balance plus the total of all contributions.

07

Why can small rate changes create big differences?

Because the rate compounds over every period, even small changes can compound meaningfully over time.

08

Is this a guarantee of performance?

No. It is a deterministic estimate based on the assumptions you enter.

09

Can I use this for savings goals?

Yes. It is useful for quick savings and investment planning under a fixed-rate assumption.

10

Why can real-world balances differ?

Real returns vary, contributions can change, and taxes, fees, or timing differences can alter results.
