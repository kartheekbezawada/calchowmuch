How to Guide

1. Enter the target and current balance

Start with the amount you want to reach and what is already saved today.

2. Set a contribution you can sustain

Consistency matters more than a one-off stretch target. Use a monthly amount you can actually maintain.

3. Add the rate and compounding only if they are realistic

Interest can shorten the timeline, but aggressive assumptions can make the plan look easier than it really is.

4. Read the result in order

Start with time to goal, then total contributions, then total interest. That shows whether the plan depends mostly on deposits or on growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What does this calculator show?

It estimates how long it may take to reach a savings target using your balance, deposits, and optional interest.

02

Does interest help?

Yes. Interest can reduce the time to goal because your balance earns growth along the way.

03

What if I already reached the goal?

The result becomes immediate because the current balance already meets or exceeds the target.

04

What if contributions are zero?

You may never reach the goal unless the current balance and interest alone are enough.

05

Does contribution timing matter?

Yes. Beginning-of-period deposits usually shorten the timeline slightly because they compound earlier.

06

Should I use monthly or annual compounding?

Use the setting that best matches the account or product you are modeling.

07

Can I use this for a house deposit?

Yes. It works well for any fixed savings target such as a deposit, emergency fund, or travel budget.

08

Why can small rate changes matter?

Because they apply over every period and compound over the full savings horizon.

09

Is this a guarantee?

No. It is a planning estimate based on fixed assumptions.

10

What should I do if the timeline is too long?

Try increasing contributions, extending the horizon, or lowering the goal.
