What Price Will You Pay After the Discount?

How to Guide

Use this calculator to turn a percentage discount into the numbers that matter: what you save and what you actually pay. With an original price of $80.00 and a discount of 25.00%, the discount amount is $20.00, so the final price is $60.00 and total savings are $20.00.

The core formula is simple: the discount is removed from the original price, which is why the final price matters more than the headline percentage when you compare deals. A smaller discount on a lower starting price can still be the better buy.

Final price = P x (1 - X / 100)

Use the real pre-discount selling price whenever possible. Inflated reference prices can make a deal look better than it is, and stacked offers usually need to be calculated one after another rather than added together.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the discount amount the same as savings? Yes. Both describe the amount removed from the original price.

Do multiple discounts add together? Usually no. They are normally applied in sequence, so the second discount is taken from the already-reduced price.

Does the calculator include sales tax? No. It shows the discounted price before tax.

Can I use decimal discounts like 12.5%? Yes. Decimal percentages work the same way as whole numbers.

What is the most common mistake? Comparing discount percentages without comparing the final prices.
