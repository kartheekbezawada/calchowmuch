What Price Will You Pay After the Discount?

Use this calculator to convert a percentage discount into a final sale price and a clear savings amount.

It is useful for shopping, promotions, coupon checks, and comparing offers when you want the answer immediately instead of working it out manually.

How to Guide

  1. Enter the original price.
  2. Enter the discount percentage.
  3. Click Calculate discount to see the final price and savings.
  4. Use the summary rows to confirm the amount saved and the amount you will pay.

At a Glance

  • Original price: $80.00
  • Discount rate: 25.00%
  • Discount amount: $20.00
  • Final price: $60.00
  • Savings: $20.00

Worked Example

If an item costs $80.00 and the discount is 25.00%, the discount amount is $20.00. Subtracting that from the original price gives a final price of $60.00.

Formula

Final price = P x (1 - X / 100)

The discount amount is found first, then removed from the original price. Savings and discount amount are the same value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a discount calculator do?

It shows the sale price after a percentage discount and how much money you save.

How do you calculate price after percent off?

Multiply the original price by 1 minus the discount rate as a decimal.

Is the discount amount the same as savings?

Yes. Both represent the amount removed from the original price.

Can the discount be a decimal like 12.5%?

Yes. Decimal percentages work the same way as whole numbers.

What if the discount is 0%?

The final price stays the same as the original price.

What if the discount is over 100%?

Mathematically the final price becomes negative because the discount exceeds the price.
