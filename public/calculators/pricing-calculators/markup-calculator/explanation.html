What Selling Price or Markup Percentage Does Your Cost Create?

How to Guide

  1. Enter cost and selling price, or switch modes if you want to solve for markup or price.
  2. Check the markup amount in currency terms.
  3. Check the markup percentage to see how much is being added relative to cost.
  4. Use basket totals when the decision involves several products together.

How Markup Works

This calculator moves between cost, selling price, and markup percentage for a single product or a basket of products. In Single Product — Cost -> Price mode, 1 product with cost of $50.00 and markup of 40.00% produces markup amount of $20.00 and selling price of $70.00.

P = C x (1 + M / 100)

Markup vs margin

Markup is measured against cost, while margin is measured against selling price, so the same sale produces different percentages.

Cost-based pricing

This makes markup a natural tool for quote building, retail merchandising, and any pricing rule that starts from cost.

Basket weighting

Total cost of $50.00 and total markup of $20.00 create a weighted basket result, so higher-cost lines matter more.

Single Product vs Basket View

Single product

A single-product result is useful when you are pricing one item or validating one quote using cost of $50.00 and price of $70.00.

Basket view

Basket view is more useful when an order mixes several items, because basket markup of 40.00% reflects the weighted total rather than a simple average.

Example Scenario

Target markup

If a retailer buys an item for $50 and wants a 40% markup, the selling price becomes $70.

Price pressure

If competition pushes the selling price down to $65, the markup falls materially even though the dollar move looks small.

Takeaway

Markup targets work best as guardrails when you decide how far discounting can go.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate price from cost and markup?

Multiply cost by one plus the markup rate as a decimal.

How do you calculate markup from cost and price?

Subtract cost from price, divide by cost, then multiply by 100.

Why is basket markup not a simple average?

Because the combined result is weighted by total cost and quantity.

Can markup be negative?

Yes. That means the selling price is below cost.

What is the most common mistake?

Using incomplete cost inputs and overstating the true markup.
