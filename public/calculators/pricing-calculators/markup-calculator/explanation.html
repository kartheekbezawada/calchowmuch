What Selling Price or Markup Percentage Does Your Cost Create?

How to Guide

Use this calculator to move between cost, selling price, and markup percentage for a single item or a basket of items. In Single Product — Cost -> Price mode, 1 product with cost of $50.00 and markup of 40.00% produces markup amount of $20.00 and selling price of $70.00.

Markup is measured against cost, which is why it is not the same as margin. In basket mode, the combined result is weighted by total cost rather than averaged row by row, so higher-cost items have more influence on the final basket markup.

The core price formula is P = C x (1 + M / 100). Across the current selection, total cost is $50.00, total price is $70.00, total markup is $20.00, and basket markup is 40.00%.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate price from cost and markup? Multiply cost by one plus the markup rate as a decimal.

How do you calculate markup from cost and price? Subtract cost from price, divide by cost, then multiply by 100.

Why is basket markup not a simple average? Because the combined result is weighted by total cost and quantity.

Can markup be negative? Yes. That means the selling price is below cost.

What is the most common mistake? Using incomplete cost inputs and overstating the true markup.
