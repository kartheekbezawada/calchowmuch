How Much Commission Will This Sale Generate?

How to Guide

Use this calculator to estimate commission from a flat rate or tiered structure. In Flat Commission % mode, total sales of $30,000.00 with rate input of 7.50% produce estimated commission of $2,250.00 and an effective rate of 7.50%.

Flat mode applies one rate to the full sale. Tiered mode prices each band separately, so only the sales inside a band earn that band's rate. That makes the effective rate the cleanest way to compare offers, test threshold changes, or check what a compensation plan really pays.

The flat-rate formula is Commission = Sales x (Rate / 100). In tiered mode, the calculator applies the same logic band by band and adds the results together. Use the same sales basis your plan actually pays on, such as gross revenue, net revenue, collected cash, or your split share, or the estimate will drift from the real payout.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do flat and tiered commission differ? Flat commission uses one rate on the full sale, while tiered commission applies different rates to different slices of sales.

What is the effective commission rate? It is total commission divided by total sales, expressed as a percentage.

Can I use this for brokerage, affiliate, or real estate payouts? Yes, as long as the payout follows a flat-rate or tiered-rate structure.

Does the calculator include caps or clawbacks? No. It calculates the base payout from the sales and rate inputs only.

What is the most common mistake? Treating the highest tier as if it applies to the whole sale instead of only the sales inside that band.
