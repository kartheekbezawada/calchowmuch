What Margin Does Your Price Create?

How to Guide

Use this calculator to measure gross margin from cost and price or to solve for the selling price needed to reach a target margin. In Cost + Price -> Margin % mode, a cost of $60.00 and selling price of $100.00 produce profit of $40.00 and gross margin of 40.00%.

Margin is based on selling price, not cost, which is why margin and markup are not the same percentage. In reverse mode, the calculator works backward from cost and target margin so you can set a minimum price instead of guessing.

The core formula is ((P - C) / P) x 100. The output is most useful when your cost base is complete, including freight, packaging, processing fees, and any normal discounting that changes the real selling price.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate gross margin? Subtract cost from selling price, divide by selling price, then multiply by 100.

How do you find price from cost and target margin? Divide cost by one minus the margin rate written as a decimal.

Is margin the same as markup? No. Margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

Does the calculator show net profit? No. It shows gross margin before overhead and other indirect expenses.

What is the most common mistake? Using markup targets when the business is really managing to a margin target.
