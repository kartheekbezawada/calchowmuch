How to Guide

What This Calculator Is For

A hire purchase plan splits repayment into regular installments plus any final balloon amount left at the end. This calculator helps you see the full structure before you focus only on the monthly figure shown in a dealer quote.

It is useful when comparing different deposits, different terms, or deciding whether a balloon payment makes the plan more affordable or simply delays too much of the cost.

How to Use the Inputs

Enter the vehicle price, your deposit, the APR, the term, and the balloon amount. The calculator then estimates how much principal is repaid during the monthly phase and how much remains due at the end.

  • Price is the financed vehicle cost before deposit is deducted.
  • Deposit reduces the amount that needs financing.
  • APR controls the interest charged during the contract.
  • Term changes both installment size and total interest paid.
  • Balloon shifts part of principal from monthly installments to the end of the agreement.

Amount Financed and Payment Logic

The core starting point is the financed amount after deposit is taken off the vehicle price. Interest is then applied across the agreement while leaving the balloon still due at the end of the term.

Amount financed = Vehicle price - Deposit

A larger balloon usually lowers the monthly installment because less principal is repaid during the monthly schedule, but the total cost can remain high because more balance stays outstanding for longer.

Worked Example

If a vehicle costs 20,000, the deposit is 3,000, APR is 8%, and the contract runs for 4 years with a 5,000 balloon, the monthly payment may look noticeably lower than a fully amortizing loan. But the agreement still leaves a large final amount that must be paid, refinanced, or settled by selling the vehicle.

That is why total payable matters just as much as the monthly installment.

How Input Changes Affect the Result

Change Typical Effect
Higher deposit Lowers financed amount, installment size, and interest exposure.
Higher balloon Lowers monthly installments but increases the amount left at the end.
Longer term Usually lowers monthly installments but raises total interest.
Lower APR Reduces borrowing cost across the full agreement.

How to Interpret the Results

Read monthly installment, total interest, balloon amount, and total payable together. Monthly installment shows affordability now. Balloon shows the end-of-term obligation. Total payable shows the full price of using the finance agreement instead of paying cash.

If the balloon looks difficult to clear at the end, test a smaller balloon or higher deposit before assuming the current monthly payment is sustainable.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Looking only at monthly installments and ignoring the balloon.
  • Choosing a longer term without checking the extra interest cost.
  • Forgetting that total payable includes deposit as well as monthly installments.
  • Assuming the final balloon can always be refinanced cheaply later.
