How it works

What this salary calculator does

This route starts with one pay amount and converts it into hourly, daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly, and annual views using the assumptions you enter for days, hours, and weeks worked.

  • Annual pay from hourly pay: hourly rate × hours per week × weeks per year
  • Monthly pay from annual pay: annual pay ÷ 12
  • Biweekly pay from annual pay: annual pay ÷ 26
  • Daily pay from weekly pay: weekly pay ÷ days per week
  • Hourly pay from annual pay: annual pay ÷ weeks per year ÷ hours per week

Worked example

If you start with $25.00 per hour, work 40 hours per week, work 52 weeks per year, and use 5 days per week, the annual pay is $52,000.00, monthly pay is $4,333.33, biweekly pay is $2,000.00, weekly pay is $1,000.00, and daily pay is $200.00.

Frequently asked questions

What does a salary calculator do?

It converts one pay amount into other pay frequencies using your own schedule assumptions.

Can I convert hourly pay to annual salary?

Yes. Enter an hourly amount, plus hours per week and weeks per year, to estimate annual pay.

Can I estimate daily and weekly pay too?

Yes. The route shows daily, weekly, biweekly, monthly, and annual views together.

Does this calculator include taxes or deductions?

No. It estimates gross pay only and does not include taxes, deductions, bonuses, or overtime.

Why do hours per week and weeks per year matter?

They determine how many total paid hours the annual pay is spread across.
