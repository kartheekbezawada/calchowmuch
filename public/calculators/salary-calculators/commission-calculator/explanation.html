How it works

What this commission calculator shows

This route keeps the commission result first, then adds total earnings and effective rate as secondary outputs. Use rate mode when commission is defined as a percentage of sales, or amount mode when the commission value is already known.

  • Rate mode commission: sales amount × commission rate
  • Amount mode commission: the flat commission value you enter
  • Total earnings: base pay + commission
  • Effective rate: commission ÷ sales amount × 100

Worked example

With $50,000.00 in sales and an 8% commission rate, the commission earned is $4,000.00. If base pay is $3,000.00, total earnings become $7,000.00.

What changes the result most

Sales amount and commission rate drive the commission figure, while base pay only changes the total-earnings support value. Flat-amount mode is useful when the commission amount is already known and you only need to back into the effective rate.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate commission?

Multiply the sales amount by the commission rate, or enter a known commission amount directly.

Can I use a commission rate or a known commission amount?

Yes. This route supports both rate-based and amount-based commission inputs.

Can I include base pay?

Yes. Base pay is optional and only affects the total-earnings support value.

Does this show effective commission rate?

Yes. The route can show the effective commission percentage when sales and commission are known.

Does this include tax deductions?

No. This route estimates gross commission earnings only.
