Formula

How salary-to-hourly conversion works

Divide annual salary by weeks worked per year, then divide again by hours worked per week.

  • Hourly rate = annual salary ÷ weeks per year ÷ hours per week
  • Weekly pay = annual salary ÷ weeks per year
  • Biweekly pay = annual salary ÷ 26
  • Monthly pay = annual salary ÷ 12

Worked example

An annual salary of $52,000.00 at 40 hours per week over 52 weeks produces an hourly rate of $25.00, weekly pay of $1,000.00, biweekly pay of $2,000.00, and monthly pay of $4,333.33.

What changes the hourly result

The same salary spread across more hours produces a lower hourly rate, and fewer working weeks push the hourly figure higher. Review both hours per week and weeks per year before using the hourly result for negotiation or comparison.

Frequently asked questions

How do you convert salary to hourly pay?

Divide annual salary by weeks worked per year and then divide again by hours worked per week.

Why does hours per week affect the result?

The same salary spread across more or fewer hours produces a different hourly rate.

Can I also see weekly and monthly pay?

Yes. Weekly, biweekly, and monthly pay appear alongside the hourly estimate.

Does this work for part-time schedules?

Yes. Enter the hours per week and weeks per year that match your own schedule.

Does this include taxes?

No. The calculator estimates gross hourly pay only.
