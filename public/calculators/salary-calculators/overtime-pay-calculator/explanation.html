How it works

What this overtime calculator does

This route isolates the extra pay from overtime hours. You supply the hourly rate, overtime hours, and multiplier instead of relying on a fixed policy assumption.

  • Overtime pay: hourly rate × overtime hours × overtime multiplier
  • Total pay: base pay + overtime pay
  • Base pay is optional and only affects the secondary total-pay output

Worked example

At $25.00 per hour with 10 overtime hours and a 1.5 multiplier, overtime pay is $375.00. If base pay is $1,000.00, total pay becomes $1,375.00.

Assumptions to review

This route does not assume a legal overtime policy. The biggest drivers are the multiplier, overtime hours, and whether base pay is entered for the same period.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate overtime pay?

Multiply the hourly rate by overtime hours and the multiplier you enter.

Why is the overtime multiplier editable?

Because overtime treatment can vary, so the route keeps the multiplier user-controlled.

Can I include total pay with overtime?

Yes. Enter base pay to see total pay alongside the overtime portion.

Does this route assume a legal overtime rule?

No. It is a user-input calculator and does not claim a universal overtime policy.

Does the result include tax deductions?

No. This route estimates gross overtime pay only.
