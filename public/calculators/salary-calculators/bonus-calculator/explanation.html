How it works

What this bonus calculator shows

This route keeps the bonus amount as the hero result, then shows total compensation and the effective bonus percent as supporting values.

  • Percent mode bonus: salary × bonus percent
  • Amount mode bonus: the flat bonus amount you enter
  • Total compensation: salary + bonus amount
  • Bonus percent output: bonus amount ÷ salary × 100

Worked example

With a $60,000.00 salary and a 10% bonus, the bonus amount is $6,000.00 and total compensation becomes $66,000.00.

Assumptions to review

Bonus percentage and base salary are the two main drivers. If your compensation package has multiple bonus layers or tax withholding rules, use this route as a gross-pay planning view, not a payroll forecast.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate a bonus from salary?

Multiply salary by the bonus percentage, or enter a known bonus amount directly.

Can I use a percentage or a flat amount?

Yes. This route supports both a percent-based bonus and a flat bonus amount.

Does this show total compensation?

Yes. Total compensation is the base salary plus bonus amount.

Can I reverse the bonus percent from a known bonus amount?

Yes. The route also shows the effective bonus percentage when salary and bonus are known.

Does this include tax on bonuses?

No. This route estimates gross bonus and total compensation only.
