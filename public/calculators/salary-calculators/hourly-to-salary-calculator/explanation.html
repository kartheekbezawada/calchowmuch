Formula

How hourly-to-salary conversion works

Multiply the hourly rate by hours worked per week and then by weeks worked per year.

  • Annual salary = hourly rate × hours per week × weeks per year
  • Monthly pay = annual salary ÷ 12
  • Biweekly pay = annual salary ÷ 26
  • Weekly pay = annual salary ÷ weeks per year

Worked example

A rate of $25.00 at 40 hours per week over 52 weeks produces an annual salary of $52,000.00, monthly pay of $4,333.33, biweekly pay of $2,000.00, and weekly pay of $1,000.00.

Assumptions to review

Hours per week and weeks per year do most of the work here. A 37.5-hour schedule or a 50-week year can change the annual estimate materially even when the hourly rate stays the same.

Frequently asked questions

How do you convert hourly pay to annual salary?

Multiply the hourly rate by hours worked per week and weeks worked per year.

What assumptions affect the result most?

Hours per week and weeks per year matter most because they determine the number of paid hours.

Can I estimate monthly and biweekly pay too?

Yes. This route also shows monthly, biweekly, and weekly equivalents.

Does this calculator include overtime?

No. It is designed for standard hourly-to-salary conversion only.

Does the result include taxes?

No. The result is a gross-pay estimate before taxes and deductions.
