Pay conversion guide

How an hourly to salary calculator turns gross wages into annual pay

Use this gross hourly-to-salary calculator to estimate annual salary from an hourly rate. Enter your hourly pay, hours per week, and paid weeks per year, then calculate to see annual, monthly, biweekly, and weekly totals side by side for fast offer comparisons.

Gross pay is the right first comparison when you want to see whether two schedules or two offers are really equivalent before taxes and deductions start to vary by situation.

How to Guide

What This Calculator Does

The calculator multiplies your hourly rate by the number of paid hours in a year. It then shows the same income as monthly, biweekly, and weekly pay.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your hourly wage.
  2. Add the hours you usually work each week.
  3. Enter the number of paid weeks in a year.
  4. Click Calculate to see annual and shorter pay periods.
  5. Compare the converted salary with other offers or budget targets.

How to Interpret Results

Result What it means
Higher annual salary You are working more hours, more paid weeks, or both.
Lower monthly pay Your yearly total does not stretch as far when spread over 12 months.
Same hourly rate, different salary The schedule, not the wage, is changing the annual result.

Start with the annual figure when you compare an hourly job to a salaried role. Then review the monthly and weekly outputs to see whether the pay rhythm fits your budget.

Formula breakdown: annual salary equals hourly rate times hours per week times weeks per year. Monthly pay is annual salary divided by 12, and biweekly pay is annual salary divided by 26.

Real-World Example

If you earn $24.00 per hour, work 40 hours per week, and work 50 weeks per year, your annual salary estimate is $48,000.00. That equals $4,000.00 per month, $1,846.15 biweekly, and $960.00 per week.

Schedule Assumptions That Change the Result

Weekly hours and paid weeks do most of the work here. A 37.5-hour schedule or a 48-week year can reduce the annual figure even if the hourly rate stays the same.

That matters when one employer offers more unpaid time off or shorter regular hours. Compare the schedule before you decide which pay offer is stronger.

Grow Your Wealth

When the converted monthly pay is higher than expected, decide where that extra cash should go before it disappears into spending. Even a small automatic transfer can build savings faster than a larger but irregular deposit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you convert hourly pay to annual salary?

Multiply the hourly rate by hours worked per week and weeks worked per year.

Does this show gross or take-home pay?

It shows gross pay only before taxes, deductions, retirement contributions, or payroll withholdings.

What assumptions change the result most?

Hours per week and weeks per year matter most because they set the total number of paid hours.

Can I estimate monthly and biweekly pay too?

Yes. The calculator also shows monthly, biweekly, and weekly pay after it estimates annual salary.

Does this calculator include overtime?

No. It is built for standard hourly to salary conversion and does not model overtime pay rules.

Does the result include taxes?

No. The result is a gross-pay estimate before taxes and deductions.

Can I use 37.5 hours per week or 48 paid weeks?

Yes. Enter your own schedule so the annual salary estimate reflects your actual paid hours.

Why does monthly pay differ from biweekly pay?

Monthly pay divides annual gross pay by 12, while biweekly pay divides it by 26.

Can I compare job offers with this?

Yes. Run each wage with its own hours and weeks assumptions to compare annual and monthly gross value.

Should I use this or the salary calculator?

Use this route when your starting point is an hourly wage. Use the salary calculator when you want every pay period at once.

Why is gross pay useful for offer comparisons?

Gross pay lets you compare the role itself before tax situations and deductions complicate the picture.

Are my inputs stored?

No. All calculations run locally in your browser - no data is stored.
