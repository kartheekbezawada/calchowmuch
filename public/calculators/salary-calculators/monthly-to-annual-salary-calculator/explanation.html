Formula

How monthly-to-annual conversion works

For a simple gross-pay estimate, annual salary is monthly salary multiplied by 12.

  • Annual salary = monthly salary × 12
  • Biweekly pay = annual salary ÷ 26
  • Weekly pay = annual salary ÷ weeks per year

Worked example

A monthly salary of $5,000.00 produces annual salary of $60,000.00, biweekly pay of $2,307.69, and weekly pay of $1,153.85 when the work year uses 52 weeks.

What changes the support values

The annual result is simply monthly pay × 12. The weekly support number changes only when the weeks-per-year assumption changes, so review that input before comparing the weekly figure to another offer or schedule.

Frequently asked questions

How do you convert monthly salary to annual pay?

Multiply the monthly salary by 12 to estimate annual pay.

Is yearly salary just monthly pay times 12?

For a simple gross-pay estimate, yes.

Can this show weekly or biweekly pay too?

Yes. Weekly and biweekly views can be shown as supporting outputs.

Does this include bonuses or overtime?

No. The route converts the monthly pay figure you enter and does not automatically include bonus or overtime assumptions.

Does it show net income?

No. It estimates gross annual salary before taxes and deductions.
