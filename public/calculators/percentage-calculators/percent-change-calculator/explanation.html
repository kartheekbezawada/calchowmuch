How Much Did the Value Increase or Decrease?

Percent change shows how far a new value moved relative to the original value. Use it when you want a normalized comparison, not just the raw difference.

How to Guide

  1. Enter the original value in A and the new value in B.
  2. Click Calculate percent change to see the signed result and direction.
  3. Check the change amount to understand the raw movement before it is converted to a percent.
  4. Use the sign to tell whether the result is an increase, a decrease, or no change.

At a Glance

  • Original value: 50.00
  • New value: 60.00
  • Change amount: 10.00
  • Direction: Increase
  • Percent change: +20.00%

Worked Example

With A = 50.00 and B = 60.00, the raw difference is 10.00. Dividing 10.00 by 50.00 gives 0.20, and multiplying by 100 gives +20.00%.

How to Interpret Your Result

A positive result means the new value moved above the original value, a negative result means it moved below the original value, and a zero result means there was no change. Use the percent result when you need a normalized comparison and the change amount when you need the raw difference in the same unit as the inputs.

Formula

% Change = ((B - A) / A) x 100

Subtract the original value from the new value to get 10.00, divide by 50.00, then multiply by 100 to reach +20.00%.

If A is zero, percent change is undefined because division by zero is not possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is percent change?

Percent change is the percentage increase or decrease from an original value to a new value.

How do you calculate percent change from A to B?

Subtract A from B, divide by A, then multiply by 100.

Why does percent change include a plus or minus sign?

The sign shows whether the change is an increase or a decrease.

What does a negative percent change mean?

A negative percent change means the new value is lower than the original value.

Can percent change be greater than 100%?

Yes, if the change is larger than the original value, the percent change can exceed 100%.

What happens if the original value is zero?

Percent change is undefined because you cannot divide by zero.

Is percent change the same as percentage increase?

No, percentage increase only applies when the value goes up, while percent change can be increase or decrease.
