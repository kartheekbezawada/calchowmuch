Buy-to-Let Guide

What does a buy-to-let mortgage calculator show?

A buy-to-let calculator estimates whether rental income can support mortgage and operating costs, and whether the property may generate positive cash flow.

Instead of looking at only rent or only mortgage, it combines property value, deposit, mortgage type, interest rate, rent, vacancy, and running costs.

What this calculator helps you answer

  • How to calculate buy-to-let cash flow.
  • What gross and net yield look like on a property.
  • Whether rental income can cover mortgage costs.
  • Why profit can be lower than expected after real costs.
  • How vacancy assumptions change return strength.
  • Interest-only versus repayment outcomes.

Core inputs

  • Property Price: purchase price or valuation.
  • Monthly Rent: expected monthly rent.
  • Deposit Type: amount or percentage input.
  • Interest Rate (%): mortgage rate.
  • Loan Term (Years): mortgage duration.
  • Mortgage Type: interest only or repayment.

Advanced inputs

  • Rent increase on/off and increase method.
  • Increase value and frequency.
  • Vacancy as percent of rent or months per year.
  • Letting agent fee as percent of rent.
  • Maintenance and other monthly costs.
  • Insurance, service charge, or ground rent assumptions.

Outputs shown

  • Estimated Net Monthly Cashflow
  • Monthly Mortgage
  • Annual Cashflow
  • Gross Yield
  • Net Yield
  • Stress Coverage

How to use the calculator

  1. Enter property price.
  2. Add realistic monthly rent.
  3. Select deposit input type and amount.
  4. Set mortgage rate and term.
  5. Compare interest-only and repayment.
  6. Add vacancy, agent fees, and running costs.
  7. Enable rent increases if modeling long-term scenarios.

What is estimated net monthly cash flow?

This is the amount potentially left after effective rent and main operating costs are deducted.

  • Mortgage payment
  • Vacancy impact
  • Letting agent fee
  • Maintenance costs
  • Insurance, service charge, and ground rent

Gross yield vs net yield

Gross yield is a quick property-level measure, while net yield includes operating cost impact.

Gross Yield = (Annual Rent / Property Price) * 100
Net Yield = (Net Annual Income / Property Price) * 100

Net yield in this calculator is treated as income after operating costs but before mortgage finance costs, because mortgage impact is already reflected in cashflow outputs.

What is stress coverage?

Stress coverage indicates how comfortably effective rent covers monthly mortgage payment.

Stress Coverage = (Effective Monthly Rent / Monthly Mortgage) * 100

A higher value usually means more resilience to cost increases or vacancy periods.

Why vacancy and running costs matter

  • Vacancy can be entered as percentage rent loss or vacant months per year.
  • Agent fees directly reduce available cash flow.
  • Maintenance and fixed charges materially change net outcomes.

What does the cashflow projection table do?

The projection table models performance over time by applying rent growth, vacancy assumptions, ongoing costs, and mortgage cost path year-by-year.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is a buy-to-let mortgage?

A buy-to-let mortgage is a loan designed for purchasing property to rent out to tenants rather than for personal occupation.

02

How does a buy-to-let mortgage work?

You borrow to purchase a rental property, collect rent, and cover mortgage and operating costs. Remaining surplus is net cashflow.

03

What deposit is usually required?

Most lenders expect a larger deposit than residential mortgages, often around 20-25% or higher.

04

Why compare interest-only and repayment?

Interest-only usually improves monthly cashflow, while repayment builds equity faster but increases monthly mortgage cost.

05

What does stress coverage mean?

Stress coverage compares effective rent to mortgage cost, helping you gauge resilience under tighter conditions.

06

How should advanced options be used?

Use advanced assumptions to model vacancy, fees, maintenance, and rent growth for more realistic planning outcomes.
