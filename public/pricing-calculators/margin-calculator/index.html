Explanation

What Margin Does Your Price Create?

Gross margin shows how much of the selling price remains as profit after cost has been covered.

This calculator works in both directions, so you can either measure current margin or solve for the selling price needed to reach a target margin.

How to Guide

  1. Choose whether you want to calculate margin or solve for selling price.
  2. Enter the cost and the additional input for the selected mode.
  3. Click Calculate margin to see the margin, price, and profit summary.
  4. Use the support rows to confirm the exact values behind the percentage result.

Worked Example

If cost is $60.00 and selling price is $100.00, profit is $40.00. Dividing that profit by the selling price gives a gross margin of 40.00%.

In reverse mode, the calculator uses the same relationship to work backward from cost and a target margin to the price you need to charge.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is gross margin?

Gross margin is profit expressed as a percentage of selling price.

How do you calculate gross margin?

Subtract cost from price, divide by price, then multiply by 100.

How do you find price from cost and margin?

Divide cost by one minus the margin rate written as a decimal.

Is margin the same as markup?

No. Margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

Can margin be negative?

Yes. If selling price is below cost, profit and margin are both negative.

What happens when selling price is zero?

Margin cannot be calculated because the formula would divide by zero.
