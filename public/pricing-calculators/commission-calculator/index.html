How Much Commission Will This Sale Generate?

Use this sales commission calculator when you need to estimate flat-rate or tiered commission on sales, compare effective payout rates, and understand how a pricing or sales plan actually pays across different deal sizes.

How to Guide

  1. Choose Flat Commission % or tiered mode.
  2. Enter total sales and the matching rate structure.
  3. Review total commission and the effective rate.
  4. Use the tier table when you want to see exactly how the payout is built.

How Commission Is Worked Out

This calculator estimates what a sale pays under either one flat percentage or a progressive tier system. In the current example, sales of $30,000.00 at 7.50% produce commission of $2,250.00, which gives an effective rate of 7.50%.

Commission = Sales x (Rate / 100)

Flat commission

Flat commission is direct: the full sale is multiplied by one rate, so the quoted rate and effective rate are the same.

Tiered commission

Each slice of sales earns the rate for that band only, which is why the highest tier is not the same as the overall payout rate.

Effective rate

In tiered mode, the clearest comparison number is the effective commission rate because it reduces the payout to one percentage of total sales.

What to Check Before You Trust the Result

  • Sales basis: Confirm whether the plan pays on gross sales, net sales, margin, or collected cash.
  • Split deals: Shared deals often reduce the personal payout even when total deal value stays the same.
  • Plan rules: Caps, accelerators, clawbacks, and quota gates can change the real payout.
  • Timing: Booking-based plans and cash-collected plans can produce the same formula but different pay timing.

When to Use This Commission Calculator

  • Sales-plan checks: Test a quoted commission rate before you approve or present a compensation structure.
  • Tier review: See how a progressive payout plan blends into one effective commission rate across total sales.
  • Pricing context: Keep commission separate from margin so you can review payouts without confusing them with gross profit.
  • Scenario comparison: Compare flat versus tiered outcomes before changing a plan, quota threshold, or commission band.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do flat and tiered commission differ?

Flat commission uses one rate on the full sale, while tiered commission applies different rates to different slices of sales.

What is the effective commission rate?

It is total commission divided by total sales, expressed as a percentage of the full deal.

Can I use this for brokerage, affiliate, or real estate payouts?

Yes, as long as the payout follows a flat-rate or tiered-rate structure.

Does the calculator include caps or clawbacks?

No. It calculates the base payout from the entered sales and rate inputs only.

What is the most common mistake?

Treating the highest tier as if it applies to the whole sale instead of only the sales inside that band.
