Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the effective annual rate (EAR)?

A: The effective annual rate is the true annual interest rate after accounting for compounding.

Q: How is EAR different from a nominal interest rate?

A: Nominal rates do not include compounding effects, while EAR does.

Q: Why is EAR higher than the nominal rate?

A: Because interest compounds multiple times per year.

Q: How do you calculate the effective annual rate?

A: By applying the EAR formula using the nominal rate and compounding frequency.

Q: What does compounding frequency mean?

A: It refers to how often interest is added to the balance each year.

Q: Is EAR the same as APR?

A: No. APR may exclude compounding, while EAR always includes it.

Q: Can EAR be used for loan comparison?

A: Yes. EAR provides an accurate basis for comparing loans.

Q: Does monthly compounding increase EAR?

A: Yes. More frequent compounding increases EAR.

Q: What happens if interest compounds daily?

A: Daily compounding produces a higher EAR than monthly or annual compounding.

Q: Is EAR useful for savings and investments?

A: Yes. It reflects the real annual return on interest-bearing accounts.
