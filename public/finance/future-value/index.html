Explanation

Future value (FV) shows how much money today can grow over time when interest is applied repeatedly. Below is a breakdown of how your inputs produce the result.

  • Present Value (PV): You start with 10,000.00 today. This is the principal that earns interest over the entire time horizon.
  • Interest Rate: A 5% annual growth rate represents the expected return on savings, investments, or the inflation-adjusted rate of appreciation.
  • Time Period: Your money grows for 3.00 years. Longer horizons produce dramatically higher future values because compound interest accelerates over time.
  • Compounding: With Annual compounding, the annual rate is split into 3 effective periods, each at a rate of 5.00% per period.
  • Contributions: You add 0.00 each period, totalling 0.00 over the full horizon. Contributions also earn compound interest.
  • Total Growth: The interest earned on both principal and contributions is 1,576.25 — this is the power of compounding.

How the Formula Works

The future value formula is:

FV = PV × (1 + r)n

Substituting your values step by step:

  1. Identify the per-period rate (r): 5% ÷ 1 compounding periods per year = 5.00% per period (as a decimal: 0.05).
  2. Determine the number of periods (n): 3.00 years × 1 compounding periods per year = 3 total periods.
  3. Build the growth factor: (1 + 0.05)3 = 1.157625.
  4. Multiply to get FV: 10,000.00 × 1.157625 = 11,576.25.

Higher interest rates or longer time horizons increase the growth factor, which raises the future value. Conversely, lower rates or shorter periods produce a smaller future value closer to the original principal.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is future value (FV)?

Future value is the amount your money will be worth at a future date after earning interest or growth over time.

02

How is future value different from present value?

Present value is today's worth of money, while future value shows what it grows into over time through compounding.

03

What interest rate should I use?

Use an expected return rate based on savings accounts, investments, or inflation assumptions relevant to your goal.

04

How does compounding affect future value?

More frequent compounding increases growth because interest is earned on previously accumulated interest more often.

05

What are regular contributions?

Regular contributions are additional amounts added each period that also earn compound interest, accelerating growth.

06

Can future value decrease over time?

With a positive interest rate, future value always increases. Only negative rates or withdrawals would reduce it.

07

What happens if interest rate is 0%?

At 0% interest, future value equals present value plus total contributions because there is no growth from compounding.

08

Can I calculate future value in months?

Yes. Switching period type to months lets you model shorter horizons with the same compound growth method.

09

Do contributions benefit from compounding?

Yes. Contributions added each period can also earn interest, which increases total future value over time.

10

Is this calculator suitable for savings goals?

Yes. It is useful for estimating savings and investment growth under fixed-rate assumptions.
