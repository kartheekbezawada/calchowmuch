Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the future value of an annuity?

A: The future value of an annuity is the total value of a series of regular payments at a future date after earning interest over time.

Q: What is the difference between an ordinary annuity and an annuity due?

A: In an ordinary annuity, payments are made at the end of each period, while in an annuity due, payments are made at the beginning of each period.

Q: Why is the future value of an annuity due higher?

A: An annuity due has a higher future value because payments are invested earlier and earn interest for a longer time.

Q: How do you calculate the future value of an ordinary annuity?

A: The future value of an ordinary annuity is calculated by compounding each payment made at the end of each period using the interest rate.

Q: How do you calculate the future value of an annuity due?

A: The future value of an annuity due is calculated by adjusting the ordinary annuity future value to account for payments made at the beginning of each period.

Q: What interest rate should be used in future value calculations?

A: The interest rate should reflect expected investment returns, savings rates, or long-term growth assumptions.

Q: Is the future value of annuity useful for retirement planning?

A: Yes, it is commonly used to estimate how regular retirement contributions will grow over time.

Q: Can this calculator be used for monthly contributions?

A: Yes, the calculator supports monthly, quarterly, and annual contribution periods.

Q: What happens if the interest rate is zero?

A: If the interest rate is zero, the future value of the annuity equals the total amount of all contributions.

Q: Is future value of annuity used for investment planning?

A: Yes, it is widely used to evaluate savings plans, investment strategies, and long-term financial goals.
