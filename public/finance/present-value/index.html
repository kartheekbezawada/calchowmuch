Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is present value (PV)?

A: Present value is the current worth of a future amount of money after accounting for the time value of money.

Q: Why is present value lower than future value?

A: Because money today can earn returns, a future amount is discounted to reflect lost earning potential.

Q: What discount rate should I use?

A: Use a rate that reflects inflation, investment returns, or the opportunity cost relevant to your decision.

Q: How does the time period affect present value?

A: Longer time periods increase discounting, which lowers the present value of a future amount.

Q: What is compounding frequency?

A: It defines how often the discount rate is applied, such as annually or monthly.

Q: Is present value used in investment decisions?

A: Yes, it is commonly used to evaluate investments, loans, pensions, and future cash flows.

Q: Can present value be higher than future value?

A: No, with a positive discount rate, present value will always be lower than future value.

Q: Does inflation affect present value?

A: Yes, higher inflation increases discounting and reduces present value.

Q: What happens if the discount rate is zero?

A: The present value equals the future value because no discounting is applied.

Q: Is this calculator suitable for loans and savings?

A: Yes, it can be used for loans, savings goals, investments, and any future cash amount.
