Explanation

Present value (PV) converts a future amount into today's value so you can compare financial options on a like-for-like basis. Below is a breakdown of how your inputs translate into the result.

  • Future Value (FV): You expect to receive 10,000 at the end of the period. This is the starting point of the discounting process.
  • Discount Rate: A 5% annual discount rate represents the cost of capital, expected inflation, or the opportunity cost of not having the money today.
  • Time Period: The money arrives in 3 years. The longer the wait, the more the future amount is discounted because each additional period compounds the reduction.
  • Compounding: With Annual compounding, the annual discount rate is split into 3 effective periods, each at a rate of 5% per period.
  • Discount Lost: The difference between the future value and the present value is 1,361.62. This represents the time-value cost — the amount "lost" by not having the money today.

How the Formula Works

The present value formula is:

PV = FV ÷ (1 + r)n

Substituting your values step by step:

  1. Identify the per-period rate (r): 5% ÷ 1 compounding periods per year = 5% per period (as a decimal: 0.05).
  2. Determine the number of periods (n): 3 years × 1 compounding periods per year = 3 total periods.
  3. Build the denominator: (1 + 0.05)3 = 1.157625.
  4. Divide to get PV: 10,000 ÷ 1.157625 = 8,638.38.

Higher discount rates or longer time horizons increase the denominator, which lowers the present value. Conversely, lower rates or shorter periods bring PV closer to the original future value.

Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is present value (PV)?

Present value is the current worth of a future amount of money after accounting for the time value of money.

02

Why is present value lower than future value?

Because money today can earn returns, a future amount is discounted to reflect lost earning potential.

03

What discount rate should I use?

Use a rate that reflects inflation, investment returns, or the opportunity cost relevant to your decision.

04

How does the time period affect present value?

Longer time periods increase discounting, which lowers the present value of a future amount.

05

What is compounding frequency?

It defines how often the discount rate is applied — annually, quarterly, or monthly — affecting precision.

06

Can present value be higher than future value?

No, with a positive discount rate the present value will always be lower than the future value.

07

What happens when the discount rate is 0%?

At a 0% discount rate, present value equals future value because no discounting is applied.

08

Can I calculate PV using months instead of years?

Yes. Switch the period type to months to model shorter time horizons with the same formula.

09

Does compounding frequency change present value?

Yes. More frequent compounding changes the per-period rate and total periods, which can change PV.

10

Is this calculator for single future cash flows?

Yes. This PV calculator discounts one future amount; annuity calculators are used for recurring cash flows.
