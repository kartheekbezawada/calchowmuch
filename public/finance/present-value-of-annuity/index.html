Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is the present value of an annuity?

It is today's value of a series of equal future payments, discounted using a specific interest or discount rate.

02

Ordinary annuity vs annuity due?

Ordinary annuities pay at the end of each period. Annuity due pays at the beginning, resulting in a higher present value.

03

What discount rate should I use?

Use a rate reflecting interest rates, investment returns, inflation, or borrowing costs relevant to the cash flow.

04

Is this useful for loans and mortgages?

Yes, PV of annuity calculations are commonly used to value loan repayments, mortgages, and lease payments.

05

What happens if the discount rate is zero?

If the rate is zero, the present value equals the total of all payments — no discounting is applied.

06

Is this useful for retirement planning?

Yes, it helps estimate the value of pensions, retirement income streams, and long-term annuity payments.
