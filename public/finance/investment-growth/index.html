Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is an investment growth calculator?

A: An investment growth calculator estimates the future value of an investment based on expected return, time, and contributions.

Q: How do you calculate investment growth over time?

A: You apply compound growth assumptions using an expected annual return and the investment period, and add contributions if applicable.

Q: What is future value in investing?

A: Future value is the estimated amount your investment could grow to after a set time period.

Q: How do monthly contributions affect investment growth?

A: Monthly contributions can increase the final balance because added deposits may also earn returns over time.

Q: What does expected annual return mean?

A: It is the assumed average yearly growth rate used for forecasting.

Q: Does compounding frequency matter for investment growth?

A: Yes. More frequent compounding can slightly increase estimated growth under the same annual return assumption.

Q: Can this calculator estimate investment returns?

A: Yes. It estimates future value and total growth based on the return rate you enter.

Q: What is inflation-adjusted future value?

A: It estimates your ending balance in today's purchasing power after accounting for inflation.

Q: What happens if the return rate is 0%?

A: Future value equals the initial investment plus total contributions.

Q: Is this investment growth estimate guaranteed?

A: No. It is a projection based on assumptions and actual market returns can vary.
