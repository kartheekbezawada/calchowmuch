Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a savings goal calculator?

A: A savings goal calculator estimates how long it will take to reach a target amount or how much you need to save each month to reach it.

Q: How do I calculate how much to save per month?

A: You can divide the remaining amount needed by the number of months, and optionally include interest to account for compounding growth.

Q: How do I calculate how long it will take to reach my savings goal?

A: Use your goal amount, current savings, and monthly contribution to estimate the number of months needed, optionally including interest.

Q: Does interest make it easier to reach a savings goal?

A: Yes. Interest can increase your balance over time, reducing the monthly amount needed or shortening the time to goal.

Q: What compounding frequency should I use?

A: Use the frequency that matches your account, such as monthly, quarterly, or annually.

Q: What if my current savings is already higher than my goal?

A: Then your time to goal is zero because you have already reached the target.

Q: What happens if my monthly contribution is zero?

A: If your goal is higher than your current savings, you will not reach it without contributions or interest growth.

Q: Is this a savings goal calculator with interest?

A: Yes. You can add an annual interest rate and compounding frequency to include interest growth.

Q: Can this help me plan for a house deposit or emergency fund?

A: Yes. You can use it for any target amount, including an emergency fund, house deposit, or holiday budget.

Q: Why do results change when I choose beginning vs end of month contributions?

A: Contributing at the beginning gives money more time to earn interest, which can slightly reduce the time to goal or the required monthly savings.
