Formula Guide

The mean is the sum of all values divided by the count. The median is the middle value in the sorted list, or the average of the two middle values when the count is even. The mode is the most frequent value, and the range is max - min.

Why compare mean and median?

FAQ

When should I use the mean?

Use the mean when you want one average value and the dataset does not have extreme outliers.

When is the median better?

The median is better when one or two extreme values distort the mean.

What if there is no mode?

If every value appears only once, the route reports that there is no mode.

Can there be more than one mode?

Yes. If several values tie for the highest frequency, the route lists them together.

What does the range miss?

Range only looks at the endpoints, so it does not describe how values behave in the middle.

Can I paste values on separate lines?

Yes. Commas, spaces, and line breaks are all accepted.
