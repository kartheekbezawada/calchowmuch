Probability Calculator

Calculate probability for single events, combined events, and distributions.

Explanation

What is Probability?

Probability measures the likelihood of an event occurring, expressed as a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain), or equivalently 0% to 100%.

When to Use This Calculator

  • Calculating odds of combined events
  • Risk assessment and decision making
  • Game theory and gambling odds
  • Quality control probability calculations
  • Understanding basic probability rules

Operations Available

  • Single Event P(A): The basic probability of event A.
  • AND (Independent): The probability of both A and B occurring when they are independent.
  • OR (Combined Events): The probability of at least one of A or B occurring, with optional overlap input.
  • Conditional: P(A|B) and P(B|A) from overlap and base rates.
  • Bayes: Update P(A|B) using P(B|A), P(A), and P(B).
  • Distribution: Binomial probability and normal approximation.

Input Definitions

  • Favorable / Total: Used for single-event probability.
  • P(A): The probability of event A occurring, as a percentage (0-100%).
  • P(B): The probability of event B occurring, as a percentage (0-100%). Only needed for AND/OR/Conditional/Bayes operations.
  • P(A and B): The overlap between events (used for OR and Conditional).
  • P(B|A): The probability of B given A (Bayes mode).
  • n, k, p: Binomial trials, successes, and success probability.

Formulas

Single Event: P(E) = favorable / total

AND (Independent): P(A and B) = P(A) Ã— P(B)

OR (Combined): P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)

Conditional: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

Bayes: P(A|B) = P(B|A) Ã— P(A) / P(B)

Binomial: P(X = k) = C(n, k) Ã— p^k Ã— (1-p)^(n-k)

Worked Example (AND)

Using defaults: P(A) = 30%, P(B) = 50%

  1. Convert to decimals: P(A) = 0.30, P(B) = 0.50
  2. Apply formula: P(A and B) = P(A) Ã— P(B)
  3. Calculate: 0.30 Ã— 0.50 = 0.15
  4. Result: 0.15 or 15%

There is a 15% chance that both events occur.

Worked Example (OR)

Using: P(A) = 30%, P(B) = 50%

  1. Convert to decimals: P(A) = 0.30, P(B) = 0.50
  2. Apply formula: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A) Ã— P(B)
  3. Calculate: 0.30 + 0.50 - (0.30 Ã— 0.50) = 0.80 - 0.15 = 0.65
  4. Result: 0.65 or 65%

There is a 65% chance that at least one event occurs.

Important Notes

  • Independence: These formulas assume events are independent (one event doesn't affect the other).
  • Conditional and Bayes modes cover dependent event relationships.
  • The OR formula subtracts overlap to avoid double-counting.

Edge Cases and Validation

  • All probabilities must be between 0% and 100%.
  • P(A) = 0% means the event never occurs.
  • P(A) = 100% means the event always occurs.
  • Results are shown as both decimal and percentage.
