Use this triangle solver to work through SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, or SSA cases with a diagram and answer-first summary in the same view. It is built for fast checking, so you can confirm side lengths, angles, area, and ambiguous-case outcomes without digging through dense trig notes.

Formula Guide

The route switches between the two standard triangle formulas instead of forcing one method for every case. The Law of Sines is useful when you know a side-angle pair: a / sin(A) = b / sin(B) = c / sin(C).

The Law of Cosines is the better fit when you know two sides and the included angle or all three sides: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). The area card then helps confirm whether the solved triangle size makes sense.

FAQ

What triangle types does this solver support?

It supports SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, and SSA triangle setups.

When should I use the Law of Sines?

Use it when you know a matching side-angle pair, especially in ASA, AAS, or SSA cases.

When should I use the Law of Cosines?

Use it for SSS and SAS cases where the included angle or all three sides are known.

What is the SSA ambiguous case?

SSA can produce two valid triangles because one side-angle combination may swing into two shapes.

Can the route show two solutions?

Yes. When SSA creates two valid triangles, both solutions appear in the answer card.

Can I enter radians?

Yes. Switch the angle unit control to radians before you solve.

Does the route calculate area too?

Yes. The answer card includes area for each valid solved triangle.

What if my inputs do not form a triangle?

The route shows a clear error state when the triangle inequality or angle rules fail.

Why does the diagram show only one triangle?

The diagram shows the first valid solution so the page stays stable, while the answer card still lists all valid solutions.

Is this useful outside classwork?

Yes. It is also useful for layout checks, surveying estimates, and quick trigonometry verification.
