Exact Value Notes

On the unit circle, the x-coordinate is cosine and the y-coordinate is sine. Tangent is sin / cos, so it becomes undefined when cosine is zero, such as at 90° and 270°.

The reference angle helps you reuse the same special triangle values in every quadrant while only changing the sign.

FAQ

What does the unit circle show?

It maps each angle to a point on a circle of radius 1, which gives sine and cosine directly.

Why are exact values important?

Exact values help you recognize standard trig results without rounding errors.

What is a reference angle?

It is the acute angle between the terminal side and the x-axis, used to match special-angle values.

Why can tangent be undefined?

Tangent is sine divided by cosine, so it is undefined when cosine equals zero.

Can I enter radians instead of degrees?

Yes. Switch the angle unit button group to radians and enter the angle there.

Do quadrant signs affect sine and cosine?

Yes. The magnitude may stay tied to the same reference angle, but the signs change by quadrant.

Can I click the diagram instead of typing?

Yes. Clicking a standard point snaps the input to the nearest special angle.

Does the route show both exact and decimal values?

Yes. Standard angles display exact labels and readable decimal approximations together.

Why do 45° and 135° share the same magnitude?

They share the same reference angle, so only the quadrant signs change.

Is this useful for trig review?

Yes. It is designed for quick recall, homework checking, and special-angle practice.
