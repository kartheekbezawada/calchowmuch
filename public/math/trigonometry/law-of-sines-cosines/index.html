Explanation

Law of Sines

The Law of Sines connects side-angle pairs: a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C). Use it when you know two angles and one side (ASA/AAS) or a side-angle pair plus another side (SSA).

Law of Cosines

The Law of Cosines generalizes the Pythagorean theorem: c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab cos(C). It is ideal for SSS or SAS triangles.

Choosing the Right Law

Auto detection checks your inputs and picks the law that fits. You can override the method if you know which one applies.

Area Formulas

Once all sides are known, use Heron's formula. If you know two sides and the included angle, use area = 1/2 ab sin(C).

Real-World Examples

These laws power navigation bearings, triangulation in surveying, and distance estimation in engineering projects.
