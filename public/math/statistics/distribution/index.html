Formula Guide

The route uses the cumulative distribution function for P(X ≤ x), the probability density function for curve height at a point, and the inverse cumulative function for quantiles. In plain language, you are asking for area to the left of a cutoff, the curve height at a cutoff, or the cutoff that matches a chosen probability.

FAQ

What is the difference between CDF and PDF?

CDF gives accumulated probability up to a cutoff, while PDF gives the height of the curve at that cutoff.

Why is a PDF value not the same as probability?

For continuous distributions, probability comes from area under the curve, not the curve height at one exact point.

When should I use a quantile calculation?

Use quantiles when you know the probability and need the matching cutoff or critical value.

Why can chi-square and F values never be negative?

Those distributions are built from squared terms and variance ratios, so their support starts at zero.

How do degrees of freedom affect the answer?

Lower degrees of freedom usually widen tails or shift the curve shape, which changes both probabilities and critical values.

Should I still check the context of the distribution?

Yes. A clean calculation does not guarantee the chosen family is appropriate for the real statistical problem.
