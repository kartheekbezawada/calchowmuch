Distribution Calculator

Calculate probabilities and quantiles for common probability distributions.

Explanation

Probability Distributions

Probability distributions describe how probabilities are distributed over the values of a random variable. Understanding these distributions is fundamental to statistical inference.

Normal Distribution

The bell-shaped curve, also called Gaussian distribution, is the most important continuous distribution in statistics.

  • Parameters: Mean (mu) and Standard Deviation (sigma)
  • Properties: Symmetric, mean = median = mode
  • 68-95-99.7 Rule: 68% within 1 std, 95% within 2 std, 99.7% within 3 std

Student's t-Distribution

Similar to normal but with heavier tails, used when sample size is small or population variance is unknown.

  • Parameter: Degrees of freedom (df)
  • Properties: Symmetric, approaches normal as df increases
  • Use: t-tests, confidence intervals for means

Chi-Square Distribution

A right-skewed distribution used for categorical data analysis and variance testing.

  • Parameter: Degrees of freedom (df)
  • Properties: Always non-negative, skewed right (approaches normal for large df)
  • Uses: Goodness of fit tests, tests of independence, variance tests

F-Distribution

The ratio of two chi-square distributions, used to compare variances.

  • Parameters: Numerator df (d1) and Denominator df (d2)
  • Properties: Always non-negative, right-skewed
  • Uses: ANOVA, comparing variances, regression significance

Calculation Types

CDF (Cumulative Distribution Function)

P(X <= x) - The probability that a random variable is less than or equal to x.

PDF (Probability Density Function)

f(x) - The height of the distribution curve at x. For continuous distributions, probabilities are areas, not heights.

Quantile (Inverse CDF)

Given a probability p, find the value x such that P(X <= x) = p. Used to find critical values.

Applications

  • Normal: Natural phenomena, measurement errors, CLT applications
  • t: Small sample means, paired comparisons
  • Chi-square: Categorical data, contingency tables
  • F: ANOVA, regression analysis, variance comparisons
