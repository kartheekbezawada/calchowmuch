Confidence Interval Calculator

Calculate confidence intervals for population proportions or means.

Explanation

What is a Confidence Interval?

A confidence interval is a range of values that likely contains the true population parameter. For example, a 95% confidence interval means that if we repeated the sampling process many times, 95% of the intervals would contain the true value.

When to Use This Calculator

  • Estimating population proportions from survey data

  • Reporting margins of error in research
  • Quality control and process monitoring
  • Political polling and market research

Input Definitions

  • Calculation Type: Choose Proportion for percentages or Mean for continuous values.
  • Confidence Level: How confident you want to be (90%, 95%, or 99%).
  • Sample Proportion (p-hat): The proportion observed in your sample (as %).
  • Sample Mean (x-bar): The average observed in your sample.

  • Sample Size (n): The number of observations in your sample.

Interpreting Results

  • A narrower interval indicates more precise estimation.
  • Larger sample sizes produce narrower intervals.
  • Higher confidence levels produce wider intervals.
  • The margin of error is half the width of the interval.

Common Misconceptions

  • A 95% CI does NOT mean there's a 95% chance the true value is in this specific interval.
  • It means 95% of similarly constructed intervals would contain the true value.
  • The interval refers to the procedure's reliability, not this particular result.

Edge Cases and Validation

  • Sample size must be at least 1.
  • Proportion must be between 0% and 100%.
  • Population standard deviation must be greater than 0.
  • Proportion bounds are clamped to [0, 1].
  • Results are formatted to 4 decimal places.
