Formula Guide

Decibels use dB = 20 × log₁₀(amplitude / reference). pH uses pH = -log₁₀([H+]). Richter magnitude uses M = log₁₀(motion / reference).

Each mode preserves the same idea: logarithms compress large ratios into smaller numbers that are easier to compare. The route keeps the active formula in the answer card so you can verify the conversion without extra navigation.

FAQ

What is a logarithmic scale?

It is a scale that converts ratios into smaller, easier-to-read numbers with logarithms.

Why do decibels use 20 × log₁₀?

That formula is used for amplitude-style ratios such as sound pressure or voltage comparisons.

What does 0 dB mean?

It means the measured amplitude matches the reference amplitude exactly.

What does pH 7 mean?

It is the neutral point between acidic and basic readings in the standard pH scale.

Why does lower concentration produce higher pH?

The pH formula uses a negative logarithm, so smaller positive concentrations map to larger pH values.

What does a +1 Richter step mean?

It means the measured ground motion amplitude is about 10 times larger than the previous step.

Can I change the reference value?

Yes. Decibel and Richter modes both let you compare against a custom reference amplitude.

Why must the inputs stay positive?

The real-number logarithm only accepts positive values, so the compared quantities must stay above zero.

Does this route convert between all three scales directly?

No. It evaluates the selected scale mode independently using the matching log formula.

When is this route useful?

It helps when you need quick ratio, acidity, or seismic-scale interpretation without extra calculation steps.
