Polynomial Operations

Perform operations on polynomials: addition, subtraction, multiplication, division

Polynomial Graphs

Explanation

What Are Polynomial Operations?

Polynomials are expressions made of terms like 3x^2 or -5x. Operations combine polynomials using algebra rules to simplify or transform them.

Supported Operations

  • Add: Combine like terms by adding coefficients.
  • Subtract: Subtract coefficients of matching powers.
  • Multiply: Distribute each term and combine like terms.
  • Divide: Use polynomial long division to find quotient and remainder.

Input Format

Enter polynomials in standard form, such as:

  • 2x^3 + 3x^2 - x + 5
  • x^2 - 4x + 4
  • -3x + 7

Worked Example (Addition)

For (2x^2 + 3x - 1) + (x^2 - x + 2):

  1. Combine x^2 terms: 2x^2 + x^2 = 3x^2
  2. Combine x terms: 3x - x = 2x
  3. Combine constants: -1 + 2 = 1

Result: 3x^2 + 2x + 1

Division Reminder

Polynomial division returns a quotient and a remainder. If the remainder is zero, the division is exact.
