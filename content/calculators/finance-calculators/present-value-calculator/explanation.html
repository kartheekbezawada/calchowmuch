Frequently Asked Questions

01

What is present value (PV)?

Present value is the current worth of a future amount of money after accounting for the time value of money.

02

Why is present value lower than future value?

Because money today can earn returns, a future amount is discounted to reflect lost earning potential.

03

What discount rate should I use?

Use a rate that reflects inflation, investment returns, or the opportunity cost relevant to your decision.

04

How does the time period affect present value?

Longer time periods increase discounting, which lowers the present value of a future amount.

05

What is compounding frequency?

It defines how often the discount rate is applied — annually, quarterly, or monthly — affecting precision.

06

Can present value be higher than future value?

No, with a positive discount rate the present value will always be lower than the future value.

Important Notes
