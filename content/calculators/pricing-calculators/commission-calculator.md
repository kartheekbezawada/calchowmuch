# Commission Calculator

This calculator shows what a sale pays under a flat commission rate or a tiered plan. Use it to price an offer, compare compensation structures, or check what a deal is likely to pay before it closes.

## How to Guide

1. Choose flat rate or tiered mode.
2. Enter total sales and the rate or tier structure.
3. Review total commission and effective rate.
4. In tiered mode, use the breakdown to see which bands create the payout.

Flat commission is simple: sales are multiplied by one rate. Tiered commission is progressive: each band of sales earns only the rate assigned to that band. That distinction matters because the top tier is not the same thing as the overall rate. A plan that advertises 13% at the top can still produce a much lower effective rate once the lower bands are included.

The effective rate is usually the best comparison number because it converts the whole payout into one percentage of total sales. That makes it easier to compare a tiered plan with a flat plan, or to test whether moving a threshold meaningfully changes pay.

Use the same sales base your plan uses. Some plans pay on gross revenue, some on net revenue, some on collected cash, and some on only part of a split deal. Caps, clawbacks, accelerators, bonuses, and quota rules can change the real payout, so treat this calculator as the base commission model unless you deliberately build those adjustments into your inputs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you calculate flat commission?

Multiply total sales by the commission rate written as a decimal.

### How do tiered commissions work?

Each portion of sales earns the rate for its own band, then the tier payouts are added together.

### What is the effective commission rate?

It is total commission divided by total sales, expressed as a percentage.

### Can I use this for real estate, affiliate, or brokerage payouts?

Yes, as long as the payout follows a flat rate or tiered-rate structure.

### Does the calculator include caps or clawbacks?

No. It calculates the mathematical payout from the sales and rate inputs only.

### What is the most common mistake?

Treating the highest tier as if it applies to the full sale instead of only the sales inside that band.
