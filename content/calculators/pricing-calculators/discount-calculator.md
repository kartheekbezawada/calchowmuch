# Discount Calculator

This calculator turns a percentage discount into two numbers that matter: what you save and what you actually pay. Use it to verify a promotion, compare competing deals, or check whether a coupon changes the price enough to matter.

## How to Guide

1. Enter the original price.
2. Enter the discount percentage.
3. Review the discount amount and final price.
4. Repeat with other offers if you want a side-by-side comparison.

The calculator applies one percentage discount to one starting price. That makes it useful for shopping, promotional pricing, and fast checkout checks. The most important output is usually the final price, not the headline percentage, because a smaller discount on a lower-priced item can still be the better deal.

Use the real pre-discount selling price whenever possible. Inflated reference prices make savings look bigger than they are. If you are testing stacked offers, run the calculator in sequence because discounts multiply rather than add.

Tax, shipping, excluded items, and minimum-order rules can change what you pay at checkout. Use the calculator to get the clean discount math first, then add any store-specific costs or restrictions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you calculate the final price after a discount?

Multiply the original price by one minus the discount rate as a decimal.

### Is the discount amount the same as savings?

Yes. Both describe the amount removed from the original price.

### Do multiple discounts add together?

Usually no. They are normally applied one after another, not summed.

### Does the calculator include sales tax?

No. It shows the discounted price before tax.

### Can I use decimal discounts like 12.5%?

Yes. Decimal percentages work the same way as whole numbers.

### What is the most common mistake?

Comparing percentage discounts without comparing the final prices.
