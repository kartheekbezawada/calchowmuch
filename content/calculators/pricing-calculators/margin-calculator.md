# Margin Calculator

This calculator shows gross margin from cost and price, or the selling price required to hit a target margin. Use it when you need a quick pricing check, a margin floor, or a cleaner way to separate profit percentage from simple markup.

## How to Guide

1. Choose whether you want margin from cost and price or price from cost and target margin.
2. Enter the cost and the matching second input for that mode.
3. Review gross margin, profit, and required selling price.
4. Re-run the numbers if you want to test cost or price changes.

Margin is based on selling price, not cost. That is why margin and markup are not interchangeable. In cost-plus-price mode, the calculator shows how much of the selling price remains after direct cost. In target-margin mode, it works backward and tells you the price needed to preserve that percentage.

The output is most useful when your cost base is complete. If freight, packaging, processing fees, or routine discounting are missing, the reported margin will be higher than your real one. Use actual net selling price and full direct cost whenever possible.

This is gross margin only. It does not include overhead, tax, financing, or channel fees unless you include them in cost. That makes it a strong pricing and screening tool, but not a full profit-and-loss model.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do you calculate gross margin?

Subtract cost from selling price, divide by selling price, then multiply by 100.

### How do you find price from cost and target margin?

Divide cost by one minus the margin rate written as a decimal.

### Is margin the same as markup?

No. Margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

### Why does discounting matter so much?

Because even a small drop in selling price can remove a large share of profit while cost stays fixed.

### Does the calculator show net profit?

No. It shows gross margin before overhead and other indirect expenses.

### What is the most common mistake?

Using markup targets when the business actually requires margin targets.
